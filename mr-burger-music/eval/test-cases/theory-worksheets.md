# Theory Worksheet Test Cases

## Intervals — Beginner

**Prompt:** Generate an intervals worksheet for beginning band students who just
learned whole steps and half steps. Keep it simple — just major 2nds and minor 2nds.

**Expected concept:** Worksheet identifying major 2nds (whole steps) and minor 2nds
(half steps) above given notes. 10–15 items. Student-facing instructions in plain
language. Answer key included. One page.

**Key checks (Range dimension N/A — no performance notes):** Items must stay within
beginner trumpet range (C4–C5) if note names are used. Answer key must be complete
and correct. Instructions must not use jargon students haven't been taught
(no "diatonic," "chromatic" without definition).

---

## Chord Spelling — Developing

**Prompt:** Generate a chord spelling worksheet for developing band students who
know major scales. Focus on major and minor triads.

**Expected concept:** Build major and minor triads from a given root. Mix of
identification (name this chord) and construction (build a Bb major triad).
10–15 items. Answer key with full chord spellings. Teacher notes on common errors
(e.g., forgetting to account for key signature).

**Key checks (Range dimension N/A):** Must include both identification AND construction
items — not all one type. Teacher notes section must be present. Answer key must
match all items exactly.

---

## Key Signatures — Beginner

**Prompt:** Generate a key signatures worksheet for beginning band students.
Focus on the keys we use most in band: Bb, Eb, F major.

**Expected concept:** Identify key signatures for Bb, Eb, F major. List the sharps
or flats in each key. Name the relative minor for each. 10–12 items. Student-facing
instructions. Answer key.

**Key checks (Range dimension N/A):** Must cover at least Bb, Eb, and F major.
Relative minor must be included. Answer key must be present and correct
(Bb major → G minor, Eb major → C minor, F major → D minor).

## Ear Training — Early Beginner

**Prompt:** Generate an ear training worksheet for early beginners. They can only
play C4–G4 and know whole and half notes. Focus on high vs. low and long vs. short.

**Expected concept:** Listening prompts and call-and-response descriptions. No notation
required — all verbal/oral. Prompts for teacher to play; student responds by describing
or echoing. 8–10 prompts. Teacher notes on how to use in class.

**Key checks (Range dimension N/A):** No written notation should be required from
students — this is listening only. Teacher notes must explain how to deliver the
activity. Prompts must stay within the stated early beginner context.
