# Jazz Leadsheet Test Cases

## Beginner — 12-bar blues

**Prompt:** Generate a beginner jazz leadsheet for trumpet students who know basic
fingerings and can read quarter and half notes. Something slow and bluesy in Bb.

**Expected concept:** 12-bar blues form in Bb (written key: C for Bb trumpet).
Chord symbols: Bb7, Eb7, F7. Simple stepwise melody using quarter and half notes,
range C4–C5. Slow swing feel, around 60–80 BPM. Score-writer handoff included.

**Key checks:** 12-bar form must be explicit (bar numbers or labeled). All chord
symbols must be dominant 7ths only — no maj7 or min7. Melody must stay within
C4–C5 written range. Score-writer handoff block must be present.

---

## Advanced — 12-bar blues with ii-V-I

**Prompt:** Generate an advanced jazz leadsheet for a developing trumpet player
in a school jazz ensemble. Medium swing blues in Bb with some chord substitutions.

**Expected concept:** 12-bar blues with ii-V-I turnaround (Cm7–F7–Bb7 or similar).
Chords include maj7, min7, dom7. Melody has leaps, syncopation, range up to F5.
Medium swing, 120–140 BPM. Score-writer handoff included.

**Key checks:** Must use at least two chord qualities (not all dom7). Melody must
include at least one rhythmic syncopation or tie. Range may extend to F5 but not above.
Score-writer handoff block must be present.

## Beginner — slow blues, ear training emphasis

**Prompt:** Generate a beginner jazz leadsheet designed for a student who is also
working on their ear. Call-and-response structure if possible. Very slow blues feel.

**Expected concept:** 12-bar blues, very slow (50–60 BPM), with call-and-response
structure indicated in teaching notes. Simple melody, room for improvisation on the
response bars. Range C4–C5. Teaching notes mention listening and phrasing.

**Key checks:** Teaching notes must mention ear training or listening. Call-and-response
or improvisation prompt must be indicated somehow. Range stays C4–C5.
