### Warm-Up — Early Beginner
**Prompt:** Generate a 5-minute warm-up for early beginner trumpet players focused on open tones G4 and C4 only.
**Scores:** Range: 3 | Specificity: 3 | Pedagogy: 3 | Level: 3 | Avg: 3.00

**Output:**

Title: Open Tone Warm-Up — G4 and C4
Concept: Long tone development on open tones only (no valve work)
Level: Early beginner
Duration: ~5 minutes
Instrument: Bb trumpet

**Important:** G4 and C4 are both open (no valves). This warm-up requires no fingering changes — students focus entirely on embouchure and breath.

**Sequence:**

**Round 1 — G4 (start here; G speaks more easily for most beginners)**
- Set metronome to 60.
- Play G4 as a whole note (4 beats). Say "tu" to start.
- Play softly — mp or less. Never force.
- Rest 4 counts (whole rest).
- Repeat 4 times total.

**Round 2 — C4**
- Play C4 as a whole note (4 beats). Say "tu."
- Play softly. C4 requires more air support and a slightly more relaxed embouchure than G4.
- Rest 4 counts.
- Repeat 4 times total.

**Round 3 — Alternating G4 and C4**
- G4 whole note → rest 2 counts → C4 whole note → rest 2 counts.
- Listen for consistent tone on both pitches. Do not squeeze up to G4 — let the air do the work.
- Repeat 3 times.

**Teaching note:**
- Play softly at all times. Pressing the mouthpiece harder to get a louder or higher sound will tighten the lips and produce a worse tone. Soft playing builds embouchure strength correctly.
- Rest when you feel any lip fatigue. This warm-up is designed to stop before fatigue sets in.
- Total time: approximately 5 minutes at metronome 60.

### Warm-Up — Developing
**Prompt:** Generate a full 15-minute warm-up sequence for developing trumpet players covering long tones, flexibility, scale, and a short chorale.
**Scores:** Range: 3 | Specificity: 3 | Pedagogy: 3 | Level: 3 | Avg: 3.00

**Output:**

Title: 15-Minute Developing Warm-Up — Full Sequence
Level: Developing
Instrument: Bb trumpet
Duration: ~15 minutes total

**Section 1: Long Tones (~3 minutes)**

Metronome at 60. Play softly.

- C4 whole note → rest 4 counts
- E4 whole note → rest 4 counts
- G4 whole note → rest 4 counts
- C5 whole note → rest 4 counts
- F5 whole note → rest 4 counts *(developing range extension)*

Reverse descending: F5 → C5 → G4 → E4 → C4 (whole notes with rests)

Hold each note a full 4 beats. Listen for tone centering. "tu" to start each note.

**Section 2: Flexibility / Lip Slurs (~3 minutes)**

No tonguing — slur all notes. Air changes the partial; fingers stay still (or move minimally).

Exercise A — Open slur (C4 and G4, no valves):
- C4 whole → G4 whole → C4 whole (slurred, no tongue after first attack)
- Rest 4 counts. Repeat 3 times.

Exercise B — Partial shift (G4 to C5, no valve change):
- G4 whole → C5 whole → G4 whole (slurred — embouchure shift only)
- Rest 4 counts. Repeat 3 times.

*Reference: Young Ensemble Warm-ups, Flexibility 1 (McAllister)*

**Section 3: Bb Scale with Eighth Notes (~5 minutes)**

Written C major for Bb trumpet = concert Bb major. Metronome at 60, working up to 80.

Ascending: C4 D4 E4 F4 G4 A4 B4 C5 (all eighth notes)
Descending: C5 B4 A4 G4 F4 E4 D4 C4 (all eighth notes)

Then extend to developing range: C4 ascending through C5 to D5 E5 F5 → descend back.

Articulation: tongue all, then repeat slurred in groups of 4.

**Section 4: Chorale (~4 minutes)**

Title: Short Section Chorale
Soprano melody (Bb trumpet, written pitch):
- Bar 1: C4 quarter, D4 quarter, E4 half
- Bar 2: F4 whole note
- Bar 3: G4 quarter, A4 quarter, G4 half
- Bar 4: C5 whole note *(breathe after)*

Repeat chorale 2–3 times. Focus: blend and intonation.
*Reference: Band Basics in Bb, Chorale section (McAllister)*

**Duration check:** 3 + 3 + 5 + 4 = 15 minutes total.

**Teaching notes:**
- Sections must stay in this order: long tones → flexibility → scale → chorale. Never reverse.
- Flexibility section is the most commonly skipped — do not skip it. It teaches the embouchure to shift partials, which is foundational for range development above C5.
