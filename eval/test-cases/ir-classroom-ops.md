# ir-classroom-ops Smoke Test Cases

## Test Case 5: observation-prep

**Skill:** `ir-classroom-ops/skills/observation-prep/skill.md`

**Prompt:** I have a formal observation on Tuesday, March 25. It's Period 3, Day 4 of a
unit on "The Other Wes Moore" (informational text / central idea — ELA.10.R.2.1). The
lesson focus is annotating for central idea using the CUBES strategy. I have 3 ESOL
students (2 Entering, 1 Developing) and 2 students with IEPs (extended time, preferential
seating).

**Expected concept:** A one-page observation reference sheet with all required sections filled
in with specific details from the prompt.

**Key checks:**
- Header includes date (March 25), period (3), day (Day 4), BEST code (R.2.1)
- Objective is specific to central idea + CUBES annotation
- Teacher and student actions are detailed, not generic
- ESOL section differentiates by level (Entering vs. Developing)
- IEP accommodations listed (extended time, preferential seating)
- Danielson or Marzano alignment present
- Output path follows the specified pattern

## Test Case 6: sub-folder-builder

**Skill:** `ir-classroom-ops/skills/sub-folder-builder/skill.md`

**Prompt:** Build my substitute teacher folder. I teach periods 2, 3, 5, and 6.
Period 2 has 28 students, Period 3 has 25 (including 4 ESOL), Period 5 has 30,
Period 6 has 22. We use a 3-station IR rotation. The school day runs 7:30–2:30
with lunch at 11:45. My emergency contact is the front office at ext. 100 and
my department chair Mrs. Rodriguez at ext. 204.

**Expected concept:** A complete 10-section substitute folder with all provided details
integrated — not a template with blanks.

**Key checks:**
- All 10 sections present (cover letter through daily sub plans location)
- Class rosters reference all 4 periods with correct student counts
- Schedule includes actual times (7:30–2:30, lunch 11:45)
- Emergency contacts include ext. 100 and Mrs. Rodriguez ext. 204
- IR-specific section explains the 3-station rotation in plain language
- Behavior management section is detailed (not just "follow school policy")
- No IR jargon left unexplained for the substitute
