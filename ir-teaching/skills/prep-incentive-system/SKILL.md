# PREP INCENTIVE SYSTEM
**Optional Prep Work + Reward-Based Motivation**

---

## PURPOSE

This system motivates student engagement through optional prep work that earns tangible rewards (class tickets or bonus points). It addresses:
- Low student motivation ("Why should I do homework?")
- Equity concerns (students without tech access can still pass)
- Teacher workload (don't manage two different lesson tracks)
- Accountability (students who prep get visible benefits)

**Key Principle:** Prep work is OPTIONAL but highly incentivized. Students can pass without doing it, but those who prep have easier, more successful experiences in class.

---

## SYSTEM OVERVIEW

### The 3 Prep Activities (Per Unit/Cycle)

**Structure:** 3 optional Schoology assignments across the 6-day cycle
**Total Time:** 60 minutes (20-25 min each for Prep 1-2, 15-20 min for Prep 3)
**Total Value:** 15 bonus points OR 6 class tickets per unit

---

### PREP 1: Building Background & Vocabulary
**Timing:** Assigned before Days 1-2, due before Day 1 class
**Duration:** 20-25 minutes
**Value:** 5 bonus points OR 2 class tickets

**Components:**
1. **Background Research (10 min)**
   - Watch 3-5 minute video about context (author, historical period, topic)
   - Read 1-page background article
   - Complete guided notes template with 5-8 questions:
     - "What is this text about?"
     - "Who is the author/speaker?"
     - "When/where was this written?"
     - "What's the historical/cultural context?"
     - "Why does this text matter?"

2. **Vocabulary Preview (10-15 min)**
   - Encounter 10-15 key vocabulary words from the unit text
   - Use context clues to predict meanings
   - Match words to definitions
   - Write 3-5 words in original sentences

**Schoology Format:**
- Combination assignment (video embed + article + fillable form)
- Auto-gradable where possible (matching, multiple choice)
- Short answer for guided notes (teacher checks completion)

**What Students Gain:**
- Enter Day 1 class already knowing the context
- Recognize vocabulary when reading text in class
- Feel confident during discussions (they have prior knowledge)
- Finish Day 1-2 work faster (already familiar with content)

---

### PREP 2: Guided Reading & Analysis
**Timing:** Assigned before Days 3-4, due before Day 3 class
**Duration:** 20-25 minutes
**Value:** 5 bonus points OR 2 class tickets

**Components:**
1. **First Read of Text (12-15 min)**
   - Read the full unit text (or substantial excerpt)
   - Annotate while reading using annotation code
   - Highlight/mark:
     - ! = Surprising/interesting
     - ? = Confusing
     - * = Important
     - ^ = Evidence to use

2. **Guided Analysis Questions (8-10 min)**
   - Answer 8-12 guided questions that scaffold benchmark understanding
   - Questions range from comprehension to analysis:
     - DOK 1-2: "What is the main idea of paragraph 3?"
     - DOK 2-3: "Find 3 places where the author's purpose is clear. Copy the sentence and explain."
     - DOK 3: "How does the author use rhetoric to persuade the audience?"
   - Require text evidence (copy sentences, cite paragraph numbers)

**Schoology Format:**
- Text discussion assignment (students submit written responses)
- Attach PDF of text with annotation instructions
- Short answer responses (teacher checks for effort and text evidence)

**What Students Gain:**
- Already read the text before class (not seeing it for first time)
- Have annotations ready to reference during class discussions
- Can jump directly to analysis in class (already understand content)
- Organizer work in class is REVISION (not creation from scratch)

---

### PREP 3: Assessment Practice
**Timing:** Assigned before Days 5-6, due before Day 5 class
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Value:** 5 bonus points OR 2 class tickets

**Components:**
1. **MC Practice Quiz (10-12 min)**
   - 10-12 multiple choice questions (DOK 2-3 level)
   - Questions about text, benchmark skills, vocabulary
   - Auto-graded with immediate feedback
   - Students see correct answers and explanations

2. **Constructed Response Planning (5-8 min)**
   - Read the CR prompt for the unit assessment
   - Complete graphic organizer or CER planning sheet:
     - Claim: What's your answer to the prompt?
     - Evidence: Find 2-3 quotes from text
     - Reasoning: How does evidence support your claim?
   - Submit photo/screenshot of planning notes

**Schoology Format:**
- MC quiz (auto-graded, students can see feedback after submission)
- Separate text submission for CR planning (teacher checks completion)

**What Students Gain:**
- Practice with question types before actual assessment
- Identify areas of confusion (can ask teacher before test)
- CR writing in class is easier (already have plan/evidence ready)
- Less test anxiety (they've seen similar questions already)

## REWARD SYSTEM: Class Tickets vs. Bonus Points

### Student Choice Model

**When students complete prep work, they choose ONE reward path:**

**Option A: Bonus Points (Academic Focus)**
- Prep work submitted to Schoology gradebook
- Earns 5 bonus points per assignment (15 total per unit)
- Boosts overall grade (can turn B into A)
- Good for students focused on GPA

**Option B: Class Tickets (Privilege Focus)**
- Prep work shown to teacher (proof of completion)
- Earns 2 class tickets per assignment (6 total per unit)
- Tickets buy privileges (bathroom, phone time, homework passes)
- Good for students motivated by autonomy/freedom

**Students CANNOT double-dip** (no bonus points AND tickets for same work)

---

### The Reward Menu: What Tickets Buy

| Privilege | Cost | Appeal Factor |
|-----------|:----:|---------------|
| **Bathroom Pass** (no questions asked) | 1 ticket | Freedom, privacy |
| **Seat Choice** (pick seat for the day) | 1 ticket | Sit with friends |
| **Phone Time** (5 min at end of class) | 2 tickets | High appeal! |
| **Extra Time** (24-hour extension) | 2 tickets | Stress relief |
| **Homework Pass** (skip one assignment) | 3 tickets | Workload management |
| **Quiz Retake** (replace lowest score) | 4 tickets | Grade recovery |
| **Lunch with Teacher** (Friday pizza) | 5 tickets | Relationship + food |
| **Free Day Pass** (work on anything) | 6 tickets | Ultimate autonomy |

**Bonus Ticket Opportunities:**
- Exceptional effort/helping others: +1 ticket (teacher discretion)
- Perfect attendance for the week: +1 ticket
- Leading class discussion: +1 ticket

---

### Tracking System: Punch Cards

**Physical System (Recommended):**
1. Print punch cards on colored cardstock
2. Each student receives one card at start of unit
3. When student shows completed prep, punch their card (hole punch)
4. Students keep cards in binder
5. To redeem: Student presents card, you punch out the appropriate number

**Benefits:**
- Visual progress (students see tickets accumulating)
- Simple for teacher (just punch, no digital tracking)
- Student ownership (they manage their own card)
- Hard to fake (you control the hole punch)

**Consequences for Lost Cards:**
- Students who lose cards lose their tickets (teaches responsibility)
- One warning/replacement per semester, then no more

## GRADING STRUCTURE

### Recommended Grade Breakdown

**Required Work (100 points):**
- **Quality Work: 40%** (Organizers, constructed responses)
- **Engagement: 30%** (Bellringers, in-class tasks, participation)
- **Assessments: 30%** (Mini-assessments, unit tests)

**Optional Prep Work (Extra Credit - Doesn't Hurt If Not Done):**
- Prep 1: Background & Vocabulary (5 bonus points)
- Prep 2: Guided Reading & Analysis (5 bonus points)
- Prep 3: Assessment Practice (5 bonus points)
- **Total: 15 bonus points per unit**

**Schoology Setup:**
- Create "Prep Work - Bonus" category with 0% weight
- Assignments add extra points but don't penalize non-completion
- Clearly labeled "OPTIONAL - BONUS POINTS"

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES

### Introducing the System to Students (Day 1 Speech)

**Sample Script:**

> "Here's how prep work is going to work this year:
>
> Every unit, I'll post 3 assignments on Schoology before we start each section. They're OPTIONAL - you don't have to do them to pass my class.
>
> BUT - if you DO complete them, you get to choose your reward:
> - **Choice 1:** Submit for bonus points (5 points each, can boost your grade from B to A)
> - **Choice 2:** Show me you did it, get class tickets (buy bathroom passes, phone time, homework passes, even quiz retakes)
>
> Here's what I've noticed in my years of teaching: Students who do prep work have a MUCH easier time in class. You've already seen the vocab, read the text, practiced the skills. Class feels less confusing, work gets done faster, and grades go up naturally.
>
> Students who don't do prep? They're seeing everything for the first time in class. It's harder, takes longer, and can feel overwhelming.
>
> I'm giving you the tools to succeed. You decide if you want to use them. Your choice, your grade, your consequences."

---

### Managing the System

**Weekly Routine:**

**Beginning of Unit (Day 1):**
- Post all 3 prep assignments on Schoology with staggered due dates
- Remind students in class: "Prep 1 is due before next class"
- Show punch card/reward menu on board

**Before Each Class:**
- Check Schoology: Who completed prep?
- Have punch cards or digital tracker ready

**First 5 Minutes of Class:**
- Students who completed prep come to your desk
- Show Schoology completion or choose tickets/points
- Punch cards or mark tracker
- Takes 2-3 minutes total

**Throughout the Week:**
- Remind students about upcoming prep deadlines
- Celebrate: "12 students did Prep 1 - awesome! You're going to crush today's work."

**End of Unit:**
- Tally tickets earned, announce top earners
- Celebrate students who completed all preps
- Preview next unit's prep work

---

### Equity Considerations

**For Students Without Tech Access:**
- Provide printed prep packets at start of unit (pick up from your desk)
- Students complete by hand, submit in class
- Still eligible for tickets/bonus points
- May need to complete during lunch or before/after school

**For Students With Language Barriers:**
- Provide prep work in native language (if possible)
- Allow longer completion time
- Provide vocabulary support/word banks
- Pair with bilingual peer for support

**For Students With Disabilities:**
- Follow IEP/504 accommodations
- Extended time, read-aloud, reduced length
- May need modified prep assignments
- Consult with ESE teacher for support

## HOW PREP INTEGRATES WITH IN-CLASS INSTRUCTION

### The Key Difference: Students Who Prep vs. Don't Prep

**IMPORTANT:** You do NOT teach two different lessons. Everyone gets the same instruction and does the same activities.

**The difference is in student experience:**

| Component | Students Who Did Prep | Students Who Didn't Prep |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **Day 1-2 Reading** | Already read text at home, reviewing and going deeper | First time seeing text, taking longer to read and understand |
| **Day 3-4 Organizer** | Revising and refining draft from prep | Creating organizer from scratch, need more teacher support |
| **Day 5-6 Assessment** | Practiced MC and planned CR, feel confident | Seeing question types for first time, more uncertainty |
| **Teacher Support Needed** | Minimal check-ins, extension questions | Frequent check-ins, foundational support |

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### "What if NO students do the prep work?"

**First 2-3 units:**
- This is normal - students are testing if you're serious
- Keep offering it, don't give up
- Celebrate ANY students who complete it (even just 1-2)
- Eventually some will try it and succeed, others will follow

**Strategies to Increase Buy-In:**
- Start small: Only do Prep 1 for first few units (build success)
- Gamify: "Class competition - which period does most prep?"
- Showcase success: "Maria did all 3 preps and finished her organizer in 10 minutes!"
- Sweeten rewards: Add highly desirable privileges

### "How do I grade prep work efficiently?"

**Completion-Based Grading:**
- Don't grade for correctness/quality (too time-consuming)
- Grade for completion/effort only
- Quick scan: Did they answer all questions? Did they provide text evidence?
- Takes 30 seconds per student max

**Auto-Grading:**
- Prep 1 Vocabulary: Multiple choice/matching (auto-graded)
- Prep 3 MC Practice: Quiz format (auto-graded)
- Only Prep 2 requires teacher grading (short answers)

## INTEGRATION WITH AGENTS/UNIT BUILDING

### When Building Units (Phases 1-3)

**Phase 1: Unit Planning**
- Include prep work in initial unit structure
- Identify content for 3 prep assignments:
  - What background/vocabulary do students need? (Prep 1)
  - What should they read in advance? (Prep 2)
  - What should they practice? (Prep 3)

**Phase 2: Lesson Planning**
- Reference prep work in lesson plans:
  - "Students who completed Prep 1 will already know vocabulary"
  - "Students who completed Prep 2 will have draft organizer"
  - "Students who completed Prep 3 will be ready for assessment"
- Plan differentiated support based on prep completion

**Phase 3: Materials Building**
- Create Schoology-ready prep assignments:
  - Prep 1: Video link + guided notes template
  - Prep 2: Text PDF + analysis questions
  - Prep 3: MC quiz + CR planning sheet
- Include in teacher materials folder
