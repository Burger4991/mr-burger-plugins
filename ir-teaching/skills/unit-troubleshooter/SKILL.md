# Unit Troubleshooter

## Purpose

Diagnose root causes before revising. A unit that "didn't work" can fail for many different reasons —
wrong diagnosis leads to wrong fix.

**Rule: No revision without diagnosis.**

## When to Use

- After delivering a unit where student data was disappointing
- After PM or FAST scores show a benchmark gap
- When delivery felt off — low engagement, confusion, off-pacing
- Before any data-driven revision (type D in unit-reviser)

## Step 1: Identify the Symptom

Ask the user one question at a time:

**First:** "What happened? Describe what you saw or what the data showed."

Listen for:
- "Students were confused" → comprehension/scaffold issue
- "Low engagement / off task" → text relevance or pacing issue
- "Assessment scores were low" → assessment alignment or prerequisite gap
- "PM data shows [benchmark] gap" → benchmark instruction issue
- "The lesson ran over / we didn't finish" → pacing issue

**Then:** "Which day(s) did this happen on? Or was it the whole unit?"

**Then:** "Which specific component — bellringer, organizer, assessment, writing task?"

## Step 2: Diagnose Root Cause

Map symptom to root cause using this table:

| Symptom | Likely Root Cause | Check |
|---------|------------------|-------|
| Confused on organizer | Scaffold too thin, or exemplar row unclear | Is I Do row pre-filled with full exemplar? |
| Confusion on MC questions | Distractors too tricky or STOP not taught | Were STOP categories explained before Day 5? |
| Low CR writing scores | Benchmark misunderstanding or RACE tier wrong | Is RACE/CER tier appropriate for semester? |
| Low vocabulary quiz scores | Words too obscure or context clues too weak | Are words Tier 2 with strong in-text context? |
| Off-task during independent | Tasks not self-running, directions unclear | Do student directions have micro-steps and checkboxes? |
| Lesson ran over | Too many activities, pacing not calibrated | Is each day within 1 front/back page? |
| Low PM/FAST scores | Prerequisite gaps, benchmark not directly practiced | Is the benchmark the actual standard being tested? |
| Students didn't read the text | Text too complex, no motivation hook | Is Lexile appropriate? Is Day 1 cold read structured? |

## Step 3: Propose Targeted Fix

Based on root cause, propose ONE specific fix (not a full rebuild):

- **Scaffold too thin** → "Add sentence starters to You Do row and a word bank to Day 3 bellringer"
- **Exemplar unclear** → "Rewrite I Do row with full text citation and step-by-step reasoning"
- **STOP not taught** → "Add explicit STOP mini-lesson to Day 2 TL script"
- **CR tier wrong** → "Drop to Lite tier (Restate + Answer only) for this unit"
- **Directions unclear** → "Rewrite Day 3-4 Independent directions with numbered micro-steps"
- **Pacing** → "Cut 1 bellringer item per day (2 MC only) to recover 2 minutes"

## Step 4: Route to Revision

Hand off to unit-reviser with:
- Specific change category (A-G from unit-reviser routing table)
- Exact files to modify
- Specific fix to implement

Example handoff:
```
Diagnosis: Day 3-4 organizer scaffold too thin — I Do row missing text citation and reasoning.

Route to unit-reviser:
- Change type: C (Difficulty Adjustment — add scaffolds)
- Files: StudentPacket, TeacherPlan, AnswerKey
- Fix: Rewrite I Do row with full exemplar (quote from paragraph [X], step-by-step reasoning)
```

## What NOT to Do

- Do not recommend a full rebuild unless 3+ components fail
- Do not guess the root cause — ask until you can pinpoint one day and one component
- Do not route to unit-reviser without a specific diagnosis
