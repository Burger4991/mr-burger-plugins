# Gradual Release Scripts - Teacher Talk Templates

## Purpose

Provides scripted teacher talk for each phase of the gradual release model used during Days 3-4 Teacher-Led rotations. These scripts make the analytical thinking process visible and repeatable for students.

**When to use:**
- Planning the Teacher-Led center for Days 3-4
- Preparing to model a new benchmark skill
- Training a co-teacher or substitute on IR routines
- Ensuring consistent language across units

**Related skills:**
- `ir-framework` — overall 6-day cycle structure
- `organizer-design` — the organizers these scripts are used with
- `benchmark-*` — specific benchmark content for each standard

## Gradual Release Overview

| Phase | Duration | Teacher Role | Student Role |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **I Do** | 5-7 min | Models thinking aloud | Watches, listens, annotates |
| **We Do** | 5-7 min | Guides, prompts, corrects | Responds, contributes, asks |
| **You Do w/ Partner** | 5-7 min | Circulates, monitors | Discusses, writes, collaborates |
| **You Do** | Remaining time | Checks for understanding | Works independently |

**Total:** Fits within 20-minute Teacher-Led rotation

## I Do Scripts (Teacher Models)

### Opening the I Do

**Script Template:**
> "Watch me think through this first row. I'm going to say everything I'm thinking out loud so you can hear my process. Don't write yet — just watch and listen. You'll get your turn."

**Transition to organizer:**
> "I'm looking at Row 1 on the organizer. The question asks me to [benchmark task]. Let me find evidence in the text."

### Thinking Aloud While Finding Evidence

**Script Template:**
> "I'm going to scan the text for evidence. I know I need to find [what the benchmark requires]. Let me reread paragraph [#]..."
>
> [Read relevant section aloud]
>
> "Okay, this part says '[quote].' Does this help answer the question? Let me check — the question asks [restate organizer prompt]. Yes, this quote shows [connection]. I'm going to write this down."

**Writing the evidence:**
> "I'm writing: 'In paragraph [#], the author states, [quote].' I always start with the paragraph number so I can find it again."

### Thinking Aloud While Writing Analysis

**Script Template:**
> "Now I need to explain HOW this evidence answers the question. I can't just say what happens — I need to explain WHY it matters."
>
> "My evidence was [brief paraphrase]. So what does this show about [benchmark concept]? It shows that [analysis]. Let me write that."
>
> "I'm writing: 'This demonstrates that [analytical statement connecting evidence to benchmark].' Notice I used the word 'demonstrates' — that's an analysis word, not a summary word."

### Closing the I Do

**Script Template:**
> "Let me reread what I wrote for Row 1: [read complete row]. Does this make sense? I have my evidence WITH a paragraph number, and my analysis explains HOW the evidence connects to [benchmark]. That's what proficient looks like."

## We Do Scripts (Guided Practice)

### Opening the We Do

**Script Template:**
> "Now it's our turn together. We're going to do Row 2 as a team. I'll guide you, but I need your thinking. Get your pencils ready."

### Guiding Evidence Selection

**Script Template:**
> "Row 2 asks us to [organizer prompt]. Where should we look in the text? Turn to your partner and point to a paragraph you think might have evidence. You have 20 seconds."
>
> [Wait. Scan for participation.]
>
> "[Student name], where did you look? Paragraph [#]? Let's read that together."
>
> [Read aloud or have student read]
>
> "Does this answer our question? [Student name], what do you think — does this show [benchmark concept]?"

**If student gives weak evidence:**
> "That's in the right section, but let me push you — does that quote actually PROVE [benchmark concept], or does it just relate to the topic? Let's keep looking. What about paragraph [#]?"

**If student gives strong evidence:**
> "Yes! That's strong because it specifically shows [connection to benchmark]. Everyone, write this down: 'In paragraph [#], [quote].' Make sure you have the paragraph number."

### Guiding Analysis Writing

**Script Template:**
> "Now the hard part. We need to explain HOW this evidence answers the question. Don't just tell me what happens — tell me what it MEANS."
>
> "[Student name], how does this evidence show [benchmark concept]?"
>
> [Wait for response]

**If student summarizes instead of analyzes:**
> "You told me WHAT happened. I need you to tell me WHY it matters. Try starting with 'This demonstrates that...' or 'This reveals that...' What does the evidence SHOW about [benchmark concept]?"

**If student gives good analysis:**
> "That's analysis. You explained HOW the evidence proves [benchmark concept]. Everyone write: '[student's analysis, cleaned up].' See how [student name] didn't just retell what happened — they explained what it means."

### Closing the We Do

**Script Template:**
> "Let's reread Row 2 together. [Read aloud.] Compare it to Row 1 that I modeled. Do you see the same structure? Evidence with paragraph number, then analysis explaining HOW it connects. That's the pattern."

## You Do with Partner Scripts

### Setting Up Partners

**Script Template:**
> "Now you and your partner are going to tackle Row 3 together. Here's what I expect:"
>
> "Step 1: Read the prompt together.
> Step 2: BOTH of you find evidence — discuss which quote is stronger.
> Step 3: BOTH of you write — don't let one person do all the work.
> Step 4: Read your answers to each other and check: Do you have a paragraph number? Does your analysis explain HOW?"
>
> "You have 5 minutes. Go."

### Circulating Prompts

While partners work, circulate and use these quick prompts:

| What you see | What you say |
|-------------|-------------|
| Student hasn't started | "What does Row 3 ask you to find? Start there." |
| Student has evidence but no analysis | "Good quote. Now tell me — HOW does this show [benchmark concept]?" |
| Student is summarizing | "You're retelling. Start with 'This demonstrates that...' " |
| One partner doing all the work | "I need to hear from both of you. [Partner], what did you find?" |
| Strong work | "Read me your analysis. [Listen.] That's proficient. Now check your partner's." |
| Off-task | "You should be on Row 3 right now. What's the prompt asking?" |

### Closing Partner Work

**Script Template:**
> "Pencils down. Let me hear one strong example. [Student name], read your Row 3."
>
> [Student reads]
>
> "Did you hear [specific strength]? That's what I'm looking for. If yours doesn't sound like that yet, you'll have time to fix it during Independent."

## You Do Scripts (Independent)

### Releasing to Independent

**Script Template:**
> "The remaining rows are yours. You've seen me do Row 1, we did Row 2 together, you did Row 3 with a partner. Now show me you can do it on your own."
>
> "Use the same process: find evidence, cite the paragraph, explain HOW it connects. If you get stuck, look back at Rows 1 and 2 as your model."
>
> "I'll be circulating. Raise your hand if you need me."

### Quick Check Prompts (while circulating)

- "Show me your evidence for Row [#]. Does it have a paragraph number?"
- "Read me your analysis. Did you explain HOW or just WHAT?"
- "Compare this to Row 1. Is your analysis as deep as the model?"

## Benchmark-Specific Script Adaptations

### For Theme (ELA.10.R.1.2)

**I Do evidence script addition:**
> "I'm looking for where the author DEVELOPS the theme — not just states it. Theme development means I need to find moments where characters change, conflicts reveal something, or the author sends a message through events."

**Analysis prompt:**
> "HOW does this moment develop the theme? What does it teach the reader about [theme statement]?"

### For Figurative Language (ELA.10.R.3.1)

**I Do evidence script addition:**
> "I'm scanning for figurative language — metaphors, similes, personification, symbolism. When I find one, I need to explain what it MEANS, not just identify it."

**Analysis prompt:**
> "What does this [metaphor/simile/symbol] REVEAL about [character/theme/mood]? Why did the author choose THIS comparison?"

### For Argument (ELA.10.R.2.4)

**I Do evidence script addition:**
> "I'm looking for the author's claim AND the evidence they use to support it. I also need to evaluate — is this evidence strong or weak? Is it relevant?"

**Analysis prompt:**
> "HOW does this evidence support the author's argument? Is it convincing? What makes it strong or weak?"

### For Point of View (ELA.10.R.1.3)

**I Do evidence script addition:**
> "I'm looking for clues about who is telling the story and HOW their perspective shapes what we see. What does the narrator notice? What do they ignore? What's their attitude?"

**Analysis prompt:**
> "HOW does the narrator's perspective affect what the reader understands? What would change if someone else told this story?"

## Pacing Guide

| Phase | Time | If Running Short | If Running Long |
|-------|------|-----------------|----------------|
| I Do | 5-7 min | Pre-fill Row 1, just explain thinking | You're over-explaining — trust the model |
| We Do | 5-7 min | Do evidence only, assign analysis as partner work | Narrow to one question, move faster |
| Partner | 5-7 min | Give 3 minutes, use strong example to close | Let them finish — this is where learning happens |
| You Do | Remaining | Assign as Independent rotation work | Normal — they continue in next rotation |

**If you only have 15 minutes:** Skip partner phase. Go I Do (5 min) → We Do (5 min) → You Do (5 min).

**If students struggle at We Do:** Don't rush to partner. Do two We Do rows before releasing.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Script Adjustment |
|---------|------------------|
| Students don't talk during We Do | Use cold call with pre-cue: "[Name], I'm going to ask you about this paragraph in 30 seconds." |
| All students summarize instead of analyze | Pause. Model the difference explicitly: "This is summary: [example]. This is analysis: [example]. Hear the difference?" |
| Partner work is unequal | Assign roles: "Person closest to the door finds evidence. Person closest to the window writes analysis." |
| Students can't find evidence | Narrow the search: "Look ONLY at paragraphs 8-12. Read them with your partner. Which sentence answers our question?" |
| I Do takes too long | Pre-write the evidence. Just model the thinking, not the physical writing. |
