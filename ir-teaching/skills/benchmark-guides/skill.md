# Florida BEST Benchmark Guides - 10th Grade

## How to Use This Skill
When planning units, always:
1. Identify the target benchmark (e.g., ELA.10.R.1.2)
2. Read the benchmark clarifications
3. Use Planning Card stems for questions
4. Map organizer rows to benchmark demands

## Key 10th Grade Reading Benchmarks

### ELA.10.R.1.1 - Literary Elements
**Standard**: Analyze how character development, setting, and plot interact and contribute to the overall meaning.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- How does [character]'s development reveal [theme/meaning]?
- Analyze the relationship between setting and [conflict/character/plot]
- Explain how [plot event] contributes to the text's overall meaning

**Organizer Row Examples**:
| Text Evidence | Character Action | How It Develops Plot | Contribution to Meaning |

### ELA.10.R.1.2 - Theme
**Standard**: Analyze and evaluate how the author develops and contrasts the points of view of different characters or narrators.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What theme is developed through [character/event/conflict]?
- Trace how the theme develops from beginning to end
- How does the author use [literary element] to convey theme?
- Compare themes across texts

**Organizer Row Examples**:
| Beginning Evidence | Middle Evidence | End Evidence | Theme Statement |

### ELA.10.R.1.3 - Point of View & Perspective
**Standard**: Analyze and evaluate how the author's choices in point of view create effects such as suspense or humor.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- From whose perspective is this told? How do you know?
- How would the story change if told from [different character]'s perspective?
- What does the narrator know/not know? How does this affect meaning?

### ELA.10.R.2.2 - Central Idea
**Standard**: Analyze and evaluate central ideas in informational texts.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What is the central idea of [section/text]?
- How does the author develop the central idea through [structure/evidence]?
- What details support the central idea?

### ELA.10.R.3.2 - Paraphrasing and Summarizing
**Standard**: Paraphrase content from texts to enhance comprehension.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- Summarize [section] in your own words
- What are the key events/ideas in order?
- Paraphrase the author's main argument

## Additional Key Benchmarks

### ELA.10.R.1.3 - Coming of Age & Conflicting Perspectives
**Standard**: Analyze how conflicting perspectives are developed through characters.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- How do characters' perspectives differ on [issue]?
- What causes the conflict in perspectives?
- How does the author reveal each character's viewpoint?

### ELA.10.R.1.4 - Poetry
**Standard**: Analyze layers of meaning and ambiguity in poetry.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What multiple meanings could this line/image have?
- How does ambiguity contribute to the poem's effect?
- What literary devices create layers of meaning?

### ELA.10.R.2.1 - Text Structure & Features
**Standard**: Analyze how text structure contributes to meaning.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What organizational pattern does the author use? (description, problem/solution, chronological, compare/contrast, cause/effect, sequence)
- How does this structure support the author's purpose?
- What text features guide your understanding?

### ELA.10.R.2.3 - Purpose & Perspective
**Standard**: Analyze author's purpose and perspective in historical texts.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What is the author's purpose? (inform, persuade, entertain)
- What perspective or bias is evident?
- How does purpose shape the content?

### ELA.10.R.2.4 - Argument
**Standard**: Compare opposing arguments and evaluate effectiveness.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What is the author's claim?
- What types of evidence support the claim? (statistics, anecdotes, expert opinion, logical reasoning)
- How effective is the argument? (validity, relevance, sufficiency)
- How do opposing arguments compare?

### ELA.10.R.3.1 - Figurative Language & Mood
**Standard**: Interpret figurative language and analyze mood.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What does this metaphor/simile/personification reveal?
- How does figurative language create mood?
- What emotions or atmosphere does the author evoke?

### ELA.10.R.3.3 - Adaptations of Classical Texts
**Standard**: Analyze adaptations of mythical/classical/religious texts.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What elements from the original are present?
- How has the author adapted or changed the source?
- What themes carry over or change?

### ELA.10.R.3.4 - Understanding Rhetoric
**Standard**: Analyze rhetorical devices and their effects.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What rhetorical devices are used? (repetition, parallelism, rhetorical questions, appeals)
- How do these devices affect the audience?
- What is the speaker's purpose?

## Vocabulary Benchmarks

### ELA.10.V.1.2 - Morphology (Etymology and Derivations)
**Standard**: Use knowledge of Greek and Latin roots to determine meaning.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What root/prefix/suffix do you recognize?
- How does the word part contribute to meaning?
- What other words share this root?

**Teaching Focus**: Bell ringers with prefix/root practice, cognate identification

### ELA.10.V.1.3 - Context & Connotation
**Standard**: Use context clues and analyze connotations.

**Planning Card Stems**:
- What context clues help determine meaning?
- What is the connotation of this word? (positive, negative, neutral)
- How would meaning change with a different word choice?

## Writing Benchmarks

### ELA.10.C.1.3 - Argumentative Writing
**CER Structure**:
- **Claim**: Clear statement answering the question
- **Evidence**: Specific text quotes with citations
- **Reasoning**: Explain how evidence proves claim

### ELA.10.C.1.5 - Improving Writing
**RACE Protocol**:
- **Restate**: Turn question into statement
- **Answer**: Provide your claim/response
- **Cite**: Include text evidence with quotes
- **Explain**: Connect evidence to answer
