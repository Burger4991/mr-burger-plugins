# ESOL Strategies Bank

## Daily Requirements
**Every lesson must include minimum**:
- 2+ ESOL strategies (rotate, don't repeat same ones daily)
- 1+ visual organizer
- 1+ modification for assessment/tasks

## High-Impact Strategies (Rotate These)

### 1. Partner Read with Sentence Frames
**When**: Days 1-4 during Independent time
**How**:
- Pair ESOL student with fluent partner
- Provide sentence frames for discussion
- Example frames:
  - "I think the author means _____ because _____."
  - "The evidence in paragraph ___ shows _____."
  - "This connects to _____ when _____."

### 2. Choral Reading
**When**: Days 1-2 during Teacher-Led
**How**:
- Read text aloud together as class
- ESOL students hear pronunciation + fluency model
- Reduces anxiety of solo reading
- Build confidence before independent read

### 3. Margin Annotations Guide
**When**: Days 1-2 during Independent
**How**:
- Pre-mark margins with symbols:
  - ⭐ = Important idea
  - ? = Confusing part
  - 💡 = Connection to theme
- ESOL students know exactly what to mark
- Reduces cognitive load

### 4. Vocabulary Preview with Visuals
**When**: Days 1-2 (before reading)
**How**:
- Pre-teach 5-7 essential words
- Include student-friendly definitions
- Add images for concrete nouns
- Practice in context sentences

| Word | Definition | Visual | Example Sentence |
|------|-----------|--------|------------------|
| persevere | keep trying even when hard | [image] | "She persevered through failures." |

### 5. Sentence Unpacking
**When**: Days 3-4 (for complex sentences)
**How**:
- Take complex sentence from text
- Break into chunks
- Color-code:
  - **WHO** (subject) = blue
  - **DID WHAT** (action) = green
  - **WHEN/WHERE/WHY** = yellow

**Example**:
"Despite countless failures, she refused to abandon her dream."
- **WHO**: she
- **DID WHAT**: refused to abandon her dream
- **WHEN/WHY**: Despite countless failures

### 6. Word Walls/Anchor Charts
**When**: Days 1-6 (ongoing)
**How**:
- Post key vocabulary with visuals
- Include example sentences
- Update throughout unit
- Students reference during writing

### 7. Collaborative Annotation
**When**: Days 3-4 (text analysis)
**How**:
- Small groups share one text
- Each student assigned a color
- Annotate for different purposes:
  - Student 1: Theme evidence (blue)
  - Student 2: Character actions (green)
  - Student 3: Important quotes (yellow)
- Discuss findings together

### 8. Sentence Starters for Discussion
**When**: Days 1-6 (all discussions)
**Frames**:
- Agreeing: "I agree with ___ because ___."
- Disagreeing: "I respectfully disagree because ___."
- Building: "Adding to what ___ said, ___."
- Clarifying: "Can you explain what you mean by ___?"
- Citing: "According to paragraph ___, ___."

### 9. Graphic Organizers with Examples
**When**: Days 3-6 (all tasks requiring writing)
**How**:
- Provide partially completed organizer
- Include sentence starters in boxes
- Show one completed row as model
- ESOL students fill remaining rows

### 10. Reduced Written Output
**When**: Days 5-6 (assessments)
**How**:
- Fewer questions (3 instead of 5)
- Shorter responses acceptable (3 sentences instead of 5)
- Focus on content, not length
- Oral option available

## Implementation Tips

**Rotation Schedule Example**:
- Monday: Partner Read + Vocabulary Preview
- Tuesday: Choral Read + Word Wall
- Wednesday: Sentence Unpacking + Collaborative Annotation
- Thursday: Graphic Organizer + Sentence Starters
- Friday: Reduced Output + Oral Option

**Don't**:
- Use same strategies every day (students need variety)
- Group all ESOL students together (distribute across ability groups)
- Lower rigor (maintain grade-level content, increase support)

**Do**:
- Rotate strategies to keep engagement high
- Combine visual + linguistic support
- Provide models before expecting independence
- Check for understanding frequently
