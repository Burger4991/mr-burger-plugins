# Literacy Skills Framework for IR

## PURPOSE

This framework makes **explicit** which literacy skills are being developed each day of the 6-day cycle. Every activity, rotation, and task should connect to at least one of these skills.

**Core Principle:** Intensive Reading is a literacy course. Students must know WHAT skill they're building, WHY it matters, and HOW to apply it.

---

## THE 6 CORE LITERACY SKILLS

| # | Literacy Skill | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|---|----------------|------------|----------------|
| **1** | **Vocabulary Development** | Understanding and using academic/domain-specific words | Foundation for comprehension; test words tested directly |
| **2** | **Reading Comprehension** | Making meaning from text; monitoring understanding | Core reading skill; needed for all benchmarks |
| **3** | **Close Reading & Annotation** | Analyzing text closely; marking important parts | Identifies evidence, tracks thinking, improves analysis |
| **4** | **Analytical Writing** | Explaining ideas with text evidence in writing | Required for constructed responses and essays |
| **5** | **Test-Taking Strategies** | Using strategies to answer MC and CR questions | Directly improves test performance and confidence |
| **6** | **Metacognition** | Thinking about your own thinking; self-monitoring | Makes students independent learners who own their growth |

## LITERACY SKILLS MAP: 6-DAY CYCLE

### Visual Overview

| Day | Primary Focus | Skills Taught | Skills Practiced | Skills Assessed |
|:---:|---------------|---------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **1** | Foundation Building | Vocabulary (benchmark vocab), Reading Comprehension, Annotation | Vocabulary (bellringer), Metacognition | Bellringer (formative) |
| **2** | First Read & Analysis | Reading Comprehension, Close Reading | Vocabulary, Annotation, Metacognition | Comprehension questions |
| **3** | Organizer Building | Analytical Writing, Close Reading | Vocabulary, Comprehension, Metacognition | Organizer draft (formative) |
| **4** | Refinement & Revision | Analytical Writing | All skills (integrated practice) | Revised organizer |
| **5** | Test-Taking Prep | Test-Taking Strategies (STOP, CER) | Vocabulary, Writing, Metacognition | Practice assessment |
| **6** | Assessment | All skills (integration) | Test-Taking Strategies | Mini-assessment (summative) |

**Pattern:** Skills are **taught** → **practiced** → **assessed** across the 6-day arc

## DAY-BY-DAY BREAKDOWN

### Day 1: Foundation Building

**Primary Focus:** Build vocabulary and introduce benchmark concepts

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer + Teacher-Led)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions (text vocabulary)
   - Teacher-Led: Explicit instruction of 2-3 benchmark academic words (e.g., theme, characterization)
   - Format: Definition, visual, example, gesture/memory aid
   - Student outcome: Can define and use benchmark vocabulary correctly

2. **Reading Comprehension** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach comprehension strategy: Predicting
   - Model using Reading Process Bookmark (Before/During/After)
   - Use neutral text (not unit text yet)
   - Student outcome: Can predict what text will be about based on clues

3. **Metacognition** (All Rotations)
   - Introduce/review metacognitive tools (Reading Process Bookmark, Annotation Code)
   - Student outcome: Can name strategy they're using ("I'm predicting")

**Independent Rotation:** First read of unit text with comprehension questions (MC format)
- Applies: Reading Comprehension, Annotation

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180
- Applies: Fluency, Vocabulary, Comprehension

---

### Day 2: First Read & Analysis

**Primary Focus:** Deep reading of unit text and initial analysis

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions (text vocabulary)
   - Student outcome: 10/30 vocabulary words learned so far

2. **Reading Comprehension** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach comprehension strategy: Questioning
   - Model asking questions during reading
   - Student outcome: Can generate literal and inferential questions

3. **Close Reading & Annotation** (Teacher-Led + Independent)
   - Teacher-Led: Model annotation techniques using unit text
   - Independent: Students annotate using Annotation Code
   - Student outcome: Text is purposefully annotated (not random highlighting)

**Independent Rotation:** Annotation Stations OR Socratic Seminar (menu mode option)
- Applies: Close Reading, Speaking/Listening, Collaboration

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180
- Applies: Fluency, Vocabulary, Comprehension

---

### Day 3: Organizer Building

**Primary Focus:** Analyzing text using benchmark organizer (gradual release)

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions
   - Student outcome: 15/30 vocabulary words learned

2. **Analytical Writing** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach: How to write analytical responses with text evidence
   - Model: I Do row of organizer (think-aloud)
   - Practice: We Do row (guided practice)
   - Student outcome: Can write claim + evidence + reasoning with support

3. **Close Reading** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach: How to locate best evidence (not just any quote)
   - Model: Evaluating which evidence is strongest
   - Student outcome: Can select relevant, strong evidence

4. **Metacognition** (Teacher-Led + Independent)
   - Use Self-Assessment Checklist before submitting organizer
   - Student outcome: Can evaluate own work quality

**Independent Rotation:** Complete "You Do" rows of organizer
- Applies: Analytical Writing, Close Reading, Metacognition

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180
- Applies: Fluency, Vocabulary, Comprehension

---

### Day 4: Refinement & Revision

**Primary Focus:** Revising and strengthening analytical writing

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions
   - Student outcome: 20/30 vocabulary words learned

2. **Analytical Writing** (Teacher-Led + Independent)
   - Teach: Revision strategies (adding detail, strengthening evidence, clarifying reasoning)
   - Model: Revising a weak response into a strong one
   - Student outcome: Can identify gaps in own writing and fix them

3. **Metacognition** (All Rotations)
   - Use Self-Assessment Checklist to guide revision
   - Student outcome: Can compare own work to exemplar and identify improvements

**Independent Rotation:** Peer review (Speed Dating) OR Gallery Walk
- Applies: Analytical Writing, Collaboration, Giving/Receiving Feedback

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180
- Applies: Fluency, Vocabulary, Comprehension

---

### Day 5: Test-Taking Preparation

**Primary Focus:** Explicit instruction in test-taking strategies

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions (may include 1-2 review words)
   - Student outcome: 25/30 vocabulary words learned

2. **Test-Taking Strategies - Multiple Choice** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach: STOP protocol (Silly, Tricky, Opposite, Proven)
   - Model: Think-aloud for analyzing all 4 choices
   - Practice: 3-5 MC questions with full STOP analysis
   - Student outcome: Can eliminate wrong answers and prove correct answer

3. **Test-Taking Strategies - Constructed Response** (Teacher-Led)
   - Teach: CER format (Claim, Evidence, Reasoning)
   - Model: Writing timed paragraph (8-10 min)
   - Student outcome: Can write organized CR under time pressure

4. **Metacognition** (All Rotations)
   - Reflect: Which strategies help me most on tests?
   - Student outcome: Can name test-taking strategy to use

**Independent Rotation:** STOP practice (5-10 MC questions with justification sheets)
- Applies: Test-Taking Strategies, Vocabulary, Comprehension

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180 OR Kahoot/Quizizz review
- Applies: Fluency, Test-Taking, Engagement

---

### Day 6: Assessment & Reflection

**Primary Focus:** Demonstrating mastery independently

**Literacy Skills Addressed:**

1. **Vocabulary Development** (Bellringer)
   - Bellringer: 5 MC context clue questions
   - Student outcome: 30/30 vocabulary words learned

2. **All Skills - Integration** (Teacher-Led + Independent)
   - Teacher-Led: Final review, small-group intervention based on Read 180 data
   - Independent: Mini-assessment (MC + CR)
   - Student outcome: Can apply all skills independently under test conditions

3. **Metacognition** (End of Day)
   - "I Used to Think... Now I Think..." reflection protocol
   - Weekly Reflection Form (if Friday)
   - Student outcome: Can articulate how their thinking/skills have grown

**Independent Rotation:** Mini-assessment (authentic testing conditions)
- Applies: All literacy skills in integration

**Technology Rotation:** Read 180 (final segment)
- Applies: Fluency, Vocabulary, Comprehension

## SKILL PROGRESSION ACROSS DAYS

### Vocabulary Development
- **Days 1-2:** Introduce new words (context clues + explicit instruction)
- **Days 3-4:** Apply vocabulary in writing (use benchmark words in organizer)
- **Days 5-6:** Retain and retrieve (review words, use in assessment)

**Progression:** Learn → Apply → Retain

---

### Reading Comprehension
- **Day 1:** Teach strategy (predicting, questioning)
- **Day 2:** Apply strategy during first read
- **Days 3-4:** Use comprehension to find evidence for analysis
- **Days 5-6:** Answer comprehension questions under test conditions

**Progression:** Strategy Instruction → Guided Practice → Independent Application

---

### Close Reading & Annotation
- **Day 1:** Teach annotation code
- **Day 2:** Annotate with purpose (stations, specific lenses)
- **Days 3-4:** Use annotations to complete organizer
- **Days 5-6:** Annotate independently for assessment

**Progression:** Learn Symbols → Practice with Support → Use Independently

---

### Analytical Writing
- **Days 1-2:** Model examples (I Do rows)
- **Day 3:** Guided practice (We Do, You Do w/ Partner)
- **Day 4:** Independent writing + revision
- **Days 5-6:** Timed writing under test conditions

**Progression:** Modeling → Guided Practice → Independent Practice → Assessment

---

### Test-Taking Strategies
- **Days 1-4:** Informal practice (bellringers use STOP format daily)
- **Day 5:** Explicit instruction (teach STOP, teach CER, model both)
- **Day 6:** Independent application (use strategies on assessment)

**Progression:** Exposure → Explicit Instruction → Independent Use

---

### Metacognition
- **All Days:** Ongoing skill
- Tools: Reading Process Bookmark (Days 1-2), Self-Assessment Checklist (Days 3-4), Reflection Protocol (Day 6)

**Progression:** Tool Introduction → Guided Use → Independent Use

## CONNECTING SKILLS TO BENCHMARKS

Every benchmark requires multiple literacy skills working together.

### Example: ELA.10.R.1.2 (Theme)

**Benchmark Requirement:** Analyze universal themes and their development

**Literacy Skills Required:**
1. **Vocabulary:** Must know what "theme," "universal," and "development" mean
2. **Reading Comprehension:** Must understand plot events to track theme
3. **Close Reading:** Must find evidence of theme at different points in text
4. **Analytical Writing:** Must explain HOW evidence shows theme development
5. **Test-Taking:** Must eliminate wrong theme statements (STOP) and write CR about theme (CER)
6. **Metacognition:** Must monitor understanding: "Am I confusing theme with topic?"

**Where Skills Are Taught in Unit:**
- Vocabulary (benchmark words): Day 1 Teacher-Led
- Reading Comprehension: Days 1-2 (predicting theme, tracking theme)
- Close Reading: Day 2 (annotate evidence of theme)
- Analytical Writing: Days 3-4 (organizer explaining theme development)
- Test-Taking: Day 5 (STOP for theme MC questions, CER for theme CR)
- Metacognition: All days (self-assessment, reflection)

## INTEGRATION WITH ROTATIONS

### Bellringer (10 minutes every day)
**Primary Skill:** Vocabulary Development
**Secondary Skill:** Test-Taking Strategies (STOP format daily)

### Teacher-Led Rotation (25 minutes)
**Days 1-2:** Vocabulary + Reading Comprehension + Metacognition
**Days 3-4:** Analytical Writing + Close Reading + Metacognition
**Days 5-6:** Test-Taking Strategies + Review + Intervention

### Independent Rotation (25 minutes)
**Days 1-2:** Reading Comprehension + Close Reading + Annotation
**Days 3-4:** Analytical Writing + Metacognition (self-assessment)
**Days 5-6:** Test-Taking (practice or assessment)

### Technology Rotation (25 minutes)
**All Days:** Fluency + Vocabulary + Comprehension (Read 180)
**Days 5-6 (optional):** Test-Taking review (Kahoot/Quizizz)

## EXPLICIT COMMUNICATION TO STUDENTS

**At Start of Every Day, Tell Students:**
"Today we're working on [SKILL]. You'll know you've mastered it when you can [SUCCESS CRITERIA]."

**Examples:**

**Day 1:**
"Today we're working on **vocabulary** and **reading comprehension strategies**. You'll know you've mastered it when you can define theme, characterization, and interaction AND predict what a text will be about before reading."

**Day 3:**
"Today we're working on **analytical writing**. You'll know you've mastered it when you can write a claim, find strong evidence, and explain HOW the evidence proves your claim."

**Day 5:**
"Today we're working on **test-taking strategies**. You'll know you've mastered it when you can eliminate two wrong answers using STOP and write a complete CER paragraph in 10 minutes."

## ASSESSMENT OF LITERACY SKILLS

### Formative (During Unit)
- **Vocabulary:** Bellringer scores (daily)
- **Reading Comprehension:** Comprehension question accuracy (Days 1-2)
- **Close Reading:** Quality of annotations (collect Day 2)
- **Analytical Writing:** Organizer quality (collect Day 4)
- **Test-Taking:** STOP practice accuracy (Day 5)
- **Metacognition:** Self-assessment accuracy, reflection depth

### Summative (End of Unit)
- **Mini-Assessment (Day 6):** All skills integrated
  - MC questions test: Vocabulary, Comprehension, Test-Taking
  - CR questions test: Analytical Writing, Close Reading, Test-Taking

### Tracking Growth
- **Vocabulary:** Track bellringer scores across units (improving?)
- **Test-Taking:** Track STOP accuracy (are students getting better at eliminating wrong answers?)
- **Analytical Writing:** Compare Day 3 organizers to Day 6 CR (did revision help?)
- **Metacognition:** Review weekly reflections (are students recognizing their growth?)

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### "Students don't understand WHY we're doing an activity"

**Solution:** Always name the literacy skill being practiced
- Before activity: "We're doing annotation stations to build your **close reading** skill."
- After activity: "How did that help you become a better close reader?"

### "Students say they're 'bad at reading'"

**Solution:** Reframe with specific skills
- NOT: "I'm bad at reading" (fixed mindset, vague)
- YES: "I'm working on my **vocabulary** and **annotation skills**" (growth mindset, specific)

### "I'm not sure which skill to focus on"

**Solution:** Use this framework
- Look at the day number → Check "Primary Focus"
- Look at your rotation → Check "Skills Taught/Practiced"
- Every activity should connect to at least one skill

## SUCCESS INDICATORS

✅ Students can name the literacy skill they're working on when asked

✅ Every lesson plan explicitly states which literacy skill(s) are being developed

✅ Students use literacy skill language in reflections ("I got better at finding evidence")

✅ Teachers can justify every activity by connecting it to a literacy skill

✅ Skills are taught explicitly, practiced with support, then assessed independently

✅ Data shows growth in specific skills across units (vocabulary scores up, STOP accuracy up, writing quality up)
