# Slide Deck Generation Protocol

## PURPOSE

This protocol ensures slide decks are:
1. **Visually accessible** (ESOL-friendly, readable from back of classroom)
2. **Pedagogically aligned** (supports I Do/We Do/You Do gradual release)
3. **Synchronized** (matches lesson plan and student packet exactly)
4. **Diagnostic-ready** (supports CFU checkpoints and gap identification)
5. **Efficient to create** (templates + AI prompts)

---

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Visual-First Design (ESOL Support)

**Every slide must include:**
- **1 image/icon minimum** (preferably 2-3)
- **High contrast** (dark text on light background OR light text on dark background)
- **Minimal text** (bullets, not paragraphs)
- **Visual hierarchy** (heading → subheading → bullets → image)

**Why**: 40%+ of IR students are ESOL learners who rely on visual cues to access content.

**Example (Good):**
```
[Slide Title: "What is Theme?"]
[Left side: Definition with 3 bullet points]
[Right side: Icon of lightbulb + universal symbol (globe)]
```

**Example (Bad):**
```
[Slide with paragraph of text explaining theme]
[No images]
```

---

### Principle 2: Gradual Release Transparency

**Students should SEE the scaffolding progression:**
- I Do slides: Fully completed examples with teacher think-aloud cues
- We Do slides: Partially completed with fill-in-the-blank prompts
- You Do w/ Partner slides: Sentence starters only
- You Do slides: Blank template or task directions

**Why**: Transparency builds metacognition ("I see how support decreases as I build skills")

---

### Principle 3: Chunking & Pacing

**Follow 7±2 rule:**
- Maximum 7 bullets per slide (preferably 5)
- Maximum 10 words per bullet
- One concept per slide

**Why**: Working memory limits require chunking for retention.

---

### Principle 4: Consistent Visual System

**Use consistent visual cues across all 6 days:**
- 📚 = Bellringer/Vocabulary
- 🎯 = Learning objective
- 👀 = I Do (watch me)
- 🤝 = We Do (let's try together)
- 👥 = You Do w/ Partner
- ✍️ = You Do (independent)
- ✅ = CFU checkpoint

**Why**: Consistency reduces cognitive load and builds routines.

---

### Principle 5: Accessibility Standards

**Font:**
- Body text: 24pt minimum
- Headings: 36pt minimum
- Never use all caps (harder to read)
- Font: Sans-serif (Calibri, Arial, or similar)

**Color:**
- High contrast (black on white, white on dark blue)
- Avoid red/green combinations (colorblind-inaccessible)
- Test readability from 20 feet away

**Layout:**
- 1-inch margins minimum
- Avoid cluttered slides
- Use white space strategically

**Why**: Must be readable from back of classroom for all students.

## SLIDE DECK STRUCTURE

### File Information

**File name:** `[UnitName]_D1-6_Slides_YYYYMMDD.pptx`

**Slide count:**
- **Days 1-2**: 8-12 slides per day (bellringer + vocabulary + activities)
- **Days 3-4**: 10-15 slides per day (bellringer + I Do/We Do/You Do organizer work)
- **Days 5-6**: 6-10 slides per day (bellringer + test-taking strategies + assessment)
- **Total deck**: 50-70 slides for full 6-day unit

---

### Universal Slide Sequence (Every Day)

**Slide 1: Day [#] Agenda**
- Learning objective(s) for the day
- Activities overview (bellringer → rotations)
- Benchmark addressed (student-friendly language)

**Slides 2-3: Bellringer**
- Slide 2: Display 5 MC questions (students work independently)
- Slide 3: Answer key with STOP justifications

**Slides 4+: Rotation-Specific Content**
- Varies by day (see Day-Specific Sequences below)

## DAY-SPECIFIC SLIDE SEQUENCES

### Day 1: Foundation Building

**Total slides: 10-12**

| Slide # | Content | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 1 Agenda | Orient students | Icon: 🎯 Include benchmark in student language |
| 2 | Bellringer (5 MC questions) | Vocabulary via context clues | Display full sentences with underlined words |
| 3 | Bellringer Answer Key | Review with STOP | Show correct answers + 1-2 STOP justifications |
| 4 | Benchmark Introduction | What we're learning this unit | Visual: Benchmark organizer preview or icon |
| 5-7 | Benchmark Vocabulary (3 slides, 2-3 words per slide) | Teach 6-8 key terms | Each slide: Word + Definition + Visual + Example sentence |
| 8 | Vocabulary Gesture Check | Kinesthetic reinforcement | "Show me the gesture for [theme]" |
| 9 | I Do: Benchmark Example (Neutral Text) | Model benchmark skill | Completed example with think-aloud cues in notes |
| 10 | We Do: Practice Together | Guided practice | Fill-in-the-blank or collaborative prompt |
| 11 | You Do Preview | What students do in Independent | Directions + expectations |
| 12 | Exit Ticket (optional) | Quick formative check | 1-2 questions on vocabulary or benchmark understanding |

**Key Features:**
- Heavy vocabulary focus (Slides 5-8)
- Benchmark introduction with visual
- One I Do → We Do cycle using neutral text (not unit text yet)

**Key Features:**
- Introduces unit text
- Models annotation/close reading

### Day 3: Organizer Work (I Do → We Do)

**Total slides: 12-15**

| Slide # | Content | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 3 Agenda | Orient students | Focus: Analyzing text with organizer |
| 2 | Bellringer (5 MC questions) | Vocabulary via context clues | Day 3 vocabulary |
| 3 | Bellringer Answer Key | Review with STOP | Quick review |
| 4 | Organizer Overview | Show full organizer structure | Display 4-row gradual release template |
| 5 | What We're Analyzing Today | Restate benchmark focus | "Today we analyze [theme/rhetoric/argument/etc.]" |
| 6-8 | **I Do: Complete Example (3 slides)** | Teacher models analytical process | **Slide 6:** Text evidence selection (show paragraph, highlight quote)<br>**Slide 7:** Analysis/reasoning (show completed organizer row)<br>**Slide 8:** Summary of process ("Notice how I...") |
| 9 | CFU Checkpoint #1 | Check understanding before We Do | "Turn to partner: What did I do first?" |
| 10-12 | **We Do: Guided Practice (3 slides)** | Collaborative analysis | **Slide 10:** Text evidence (project paragraph, students help find quote)<br>**Slide 11:** Partially completed organizer row with fill-in-the-blank<br>**Slide 12:** "Let's complete this together" (call on students) |
| 13 | CFU Checkpoint #2 | Check understanding before partner work | "What's the difference between I Do and We Do?" |
| 14 | You Do w/ Partner Preview | Directions for partner practice | Show sentence starters, time limit (5 min) |
| 15 | You Do (Independent) Preview | What to complete alone | Blank row, expectations |

**Key Features:**
- **Slides 6-8 (I Do)**: Fully completed with visible process
- **Slides 10-12 (We Do)**: Partially complete, collaborative
- CFU checkpoints before transitions
- Clear scaffolding progression visible

**Teacher Notes Integration:**
- Slide 6 notes: "Prediction of Pitfall: Students may pick quote without analysis..."
- Slide 9 notes: "Listen for: Can students explain the process?"
- Slide 13 notes: "If students can't distinguish I Do from We Do, reteach scaffold concept"

### Day 4: Organizer Work (You Do w/ Partner → You Do)

**Total slides: 8-10**

| Slide # | Content | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 4 Agenda | Orient students | Focus: Independent practice with organizer |
| 2 | Bellringer (5 MC questions) | Vocabulary via context clues | Day 4 vocabulary |
| 3 | Bellringer Answer Key | Review with STOP | Quick review |

**Key Features:**
- Less teacher modeling (review only)

### Day 5: Test-Taking Strategies

**Total slides: 8-10**

| Slide # | Content | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 5 Agenda | Orient students | Focus: STOP for MC, CER for writing |
| 2 | Bellringer (5 MC questions) | Vocabulary via context clues | Day 5 vocabulary |
| 3 | Bellringer Answer Key | Review with STOP | Emphasize STOP patterns today |
| 4 | STOP Protocol Review | Teach/review Silly, Tricky, Opposite, Proven | Visual anchor chart: S/T/O/P definitions + icons |
| 5-6 | **I Do: Model STOP (2 slides)** | Teacher models STOP thinking | **Slide 5:** Display MC question<br>**Slide 6:** Show STOP labels for each choice + reasoning |
| 7 | We Do: STOP Practice | Collaborative STOP analysis | Students label choices, discuss with partner, share |
| 8 | CER or RACE Review | Writing framework for CR questions | Visual: CER structure (Claim → Evidence → Reasoning) |
| 9 | I Do: Model CER Paragraph | Teacher writes exemplar | Display completed paragraph with C/E/R color-coded |
| 10 | You Do Preview: Practice Assessment | What to expect in Independent | "Today you'll practice MC with STOP + write CER paragraph" |

**Key Features:**
- Focus on test-taking strategies (not new content)
- Explicit STOP modeling
- Writing exemplar for CER

---

### Day 6: Assessment

**Total slides: 6-8**

| Slide # | Content | Purpose | Design Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 6 Agenda | Orient students | Focus: Mini-assessment |
| 2 | Bellringer (5 MC questions) | Vocabulary via context clues | Day 6 vocabulary (final 5 words) |
| 3 | Bellringer Answer Key | Review with STOP | Quick review |
| 4 | Assessment Directions | Clarify expectations | "10-12 MC questions + 1 CR question. Use STOP. Use CER." |

| 6 | Assessment Tips | Reminders | "Reread question, eliminate Silly, choose Proven, cite paragraph #s" |
| 7 | Post-Assessment Reflection (optional) | Metacognition | "What strategy helped you most today?" |
| 8 | Unit Wrap-Up (optional) | Celebrate + preview next unit | "What we learned: [benchmark]. Next unit: [preview]" |

**Key Features:**
- Minimal slides (focus is on assessment)
- Clear directions and time management
- Optional reflection for metacognition

## SLIDE TEMPLATES BY TYPE

### Template 1: Vocabulary Slide

**Use:** Days 1-2 for benchmark vocabulary instruction

**Layout:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ [WORD] (36pt, bold)                     │
│                                         │
│ Definition: [1 sentence] (24pt)         │
│                                         │
│ Example: "[Sentence from text]" (20pt)  │
│                                         │
│ [ICON/IMAGE - right side, 30% of slide] │
│                                         │
│ Gesture: [Description or image] (18pt)  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Example (Theme):**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ THEME                                   │
│                                         │
│ Definition: A universal message about   │
│ life that applies to anyone, anywhere. │
│                                         │
│ Example: "Love requires sacrifice"     │
│                                         │
│ [Image: Globe icon]                    │
│                                         │
│ Gesture: Arms spread wide (universal)  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**PowerPoint Notes Section:**
```
SAY: "Theme is NOT the topic. Topic = what story is about (love, war, family). Theme = MESSAGE about that topic. Ask yourself: What lesson applies to ANYONE?"

PREDICTION OF PITFALL: Students will say "the theme is love" (topic not theme).
REMEDIATE: "Is 'love' universal? Or is 'love requires sacrifice' universal?"
```

### Template 2: I Do Slide (Modeling)

**Use:** Days 1-4 for teacher modeling

**Layout:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 👀 I DO: Watch How I [Skill]            │
│                                         │
│ [Left side: Text excerpt or question]   │
│                                         │
│ [Right side: Completed response]        │
│                                         │
│ [Bottom: Process summary]               │
│   Step 1: [Action]                      │
│   Step 2: [Action]                      │
│   Step 3: [Action]                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Example (Theme Organizer - I Do Row):**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 👀 I DO: How to Find Theme              │
│                                         │
│ Text: "She did not stop to ask if it   │
│ were or were not a monstrous joy" (¶10)│
│                                         │
│ Analysis: The theme is "Freedom can     │
│ come from unexpected places" because    │
│ Mrs. Mallard feels joy (freedom) from   │
│ her husband's death (unexpected loss).  │
│                                         │
│ My Process:                             │
│  1. Found key moment (¶10)              │
│  2. Asked "What's universal message?"   │
│  3. Explained HOW text shows theme      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**PowerPoint Notes Section:**
```
THINK-ALOUD SCRIPT:
"Let me show you my thinking. First, I reread paragraph 10 where she feels 'monstrous joy.' I ask myself: What's UNIVERSAL here? It's not just about marriage—it's about freedom coming from unexpected circumstances. Anyone could relate to finding freedom in surprising ways. Now I need to explain HOW the text shows this. She feels joy (which = freedom) after her husband dies (which = unexpected loss). Let me write that in my organizer..."

CFU: "Turn to your partner: What was my first step?" [LISTEN FOR: "You found a key moment"]
```

```

- "The theme is love" → Remediate: "That's the topic. What MESSAGE about love?"
```

## INTEGRATION WITH LESSON PLAN & STUDENT PACKET

### Synchronization Rules

**Rule 1: Vocabulary Must Match**
- Bellringer slides (Day 1-6) must use SAME sentences and words as student packet bellringers
- Benchmark vocabulary slides (Days 1-2) must match words taught in lesson plan Teacher-Led section

**Rule 2: Organizer Examples Must Match**
- I Do slides must show SAME completed example as lesson plan script AND student packet I Do row
- We Do slides must use SAME prompts as lesson plan script AND student packet We Do row

**Rule 3: Timing Must Align**
- If lesson plan says "8-12 min I Do," slides should support that pacing (don't create 20 slides for 10-min section)
- Rule of thumb: 1-2 minutes per slide maximum

**Rule 4: CFU Checkpoints Must Match**
- If lesson plan includes CFU checkpoint, slides must include corresponding CFU slide
- Slide notes should mirror lesson plan's "Listen for" indicators

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### Pitfall 1: Slides Are Text-Heavy
**Problem:** Paragraphs of text on slides (students tune out, ESOL learners can't access)
**Fix:** Maximum 7 bullets per slide, 10 words per bullet. Use images to convey meaning.

### Pitfall 2: No Visual Hierarchy
**Problem:** All text same size, no clear focus
**Fix:** Heading (36pt+) → Subheading (28pt) → Bullets (24pt). Use bold for key words.

### Pitfall 3: Slides Don't Match Lesson Plan
**Problem:** Slides show different examples than lesson plan
**Fix:** Use exact same examples, questions, and sentence starters across all 3 files (lesson plan, student packet, slides)

### Pitfall 4: Missing CFU Slides
**Problem:** Lesson plan says "Check for understanding" but no corresponding slide
**Fix:** Create CFU checkpoint slide for every CFU mentioned in lesson plan

### Pitfall 5: PowerPoint Notes Are Empty
**Problem:** No teacher guidance in notes section
**Fix:** Add teacher scripts, think-alouds, "Listen for" indicators, and pitfall predictions to EVERY instructional slide (I Do, We Do, CFU)

### Pitfall 6: Too Many Slides for Time Allowed
**Problem:** 20 slides for a 10-minute Teacher-Led section
**Fix:** Rule of thumb: 1-2 minutes per slide maximum. If lesson plan says 10 min, create 5-10 slides max.

### Pitfall 7: No Images for ESOL Support
**Problem:** Text-only slides
**Fix:** EVERY slide must have at least 1 image, icon, or visual. Use consistent icons (📚👀🤝) for routines.

### Pitfall 8: Inaccessible Colors
**Problem:** Low contrast (gray text on white) or colorblind-inaccessible (red/green)
**Fix:** Use black on white OR white on dark blue. Test readability from back of room.
