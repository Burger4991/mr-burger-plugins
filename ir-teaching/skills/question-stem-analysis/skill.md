# Question Stem Analysis — 10th Grade Intensive Reading

## Purpose

Question stem analysis is the bridge between reading a question and knowing what to hunt for in the passage. It's the instructional heart of Day 2 in the test prep cycle and a metacognitive skill that transfers to any reading assessment. Students who can categorize a question by benchmark and predict what evidence they'll need consistently outperform students who just "read and hope."

**The core insight:** Every MC question on a reading test is asking ONE specific cognitive task. If students can name that task before they look at the passage, they read with purpose instead of confusion.

**Where this fits in the Attack the Passage protocol:** Phase 1 (Attack the Questions) uses C-U-B to mark up questions. Question stem analysis goes deeper — it teaches students WHY they're circling those key terms and HOW to use them to predict their evidence hunt.

## Question Type Categories

### Category 1: Literal Comprehension (DOK 1)

Questions that ask what the text explicitly says.

| Signal Words | What It's Asking | Hunt Strategy |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "According to the passage..." | Find a stated fact | Scan for direct match |
| "The text states that..." | Locate explicit info | Find the exact sentence |
| "Which detail from the text..." | Identify a specific detail | Box the paragraph reference |
| "In paragraph ___..." | Info from a specific location | Go directly to that paragraph |

**Benchmark connections:** These appear across all benchmarks as Part A questions or as setup for deeper analysis.

### Category 2: Inference (DOK 2)

Questions that ask what the text implies but doesn't directly state.

| Signal Words | What It's Asking | Hunt Strategy |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "What can the reader infer..." | Draw a conclusion from clues | Combine 2+ details |
| "The author most likely means..." | Interpret implied meaning | Look for context clues around the word/phrase |
| "Based on the passage, it is reasonable to conclude..." | Make a supported conclusion | Find pattern across paragraphs |
| "What is suggested by..." | Identify implication | Look for what's NOT said directly |

**Benchmark connections:** R.1.1 (character inference), R.1.2 (theme inference), R.2.2 (implied central idea)

### Category 3: Analysis (DOK 2-3)

Questions that ask HOW or WHY the author made specific choices.

| Signal Words | What It's Asking | Hunt Strategy |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "How does the author develop..." | Trace a technique across the text | Track how something builds or changes |
| "The author's use of ___ serves to..." | Identify the function of a technique | Find the technique, then ask "what does it DO?" |
| "What effect does ___ have on..." | Explain impact of a choice | Find the choice, read around it, name the effect |
| "How does ___ contribute to..." | Connect an element to meaning | Find the element, explain its role in the big picture |
| "Which best describes the relationship between..." | Compare/connect two elements | Find both elements, identify the link |

**Benchmark connections:** R.1.1 (how elements interact), R.1.3 (how POV shapes meaning), R.1.4 (how layers create ambiguity), R.2.1 (how structure supports purpose), R.3.1 (how figurative language creates mood), R.3.4 (how rhetoric persuades)

### Category 4: Evaluation (DOK 3)

Questions that ask students to judge, compare, or assess.

| Signal Words | What It's Asking | Hunt Strategy |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "Which statement best summarizes..." | Select the most accurate summary | Eliminate partial summaries |
| "Which best supports the idea that..." | Select the strongest evidence | Rank evidence by strength and directness |
| "The author would most likely agree that..." | Extend the author's position | Identify author's values, apply to new statement |
| "Which argument is most effectively supported..." | Judge argument strength | Compare evidence quality across claims |

**Benchmark connections:** R.2.3 (evaluate purpose), R.2.4 (evaluate argument strength), R.3.4 (evaluate rhetorical effectiveness)

## Benchmark-to-Question-Stem Map

This is the core reference for Day 2 instruction. Students learn to identify which benchmark a question is testing.

### Literary Benchmarks

| Benchmark | Common Question Stems | What Students Should Circle |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **R.1.1** Literary Elements | "How does [character] develop...", "What role does [setting] play...", "How does the plot reveal..." | Character names, setting references, plot terms (conflict, resolution) |
| **R.1.2** Theme | "What theme is developed...", "How does [element] contribute to the theme...", "Which detail best supports the theme..." | "theme", "message", "lesson", "meaning" |
| **R.1.3** Point of View | "How does the narrator's perspective...", "What effect does [POV] have...", "How does the shift in perspective..." | "narrator", "perspective", "point of view", "speaker" |
| **R.1.4** Poetry | "What is the effect of the ambiguity in...", "How do the layers of meaning in [line]...", "The speaker's tone shifts from ___ to ___..." | "ambiguity", "layers", "multiple meanings", "tone shift", line references |
| **R.3.1** Figurative Language | "The [metaphor/simile] in line ___ suggests...", "How does the author's use of [device] create mood...", "What mood is established by..." | Device names (metaphor, simile, personification), "mood", "tone", "imagery" |

### Informational Benchmarks

| Benchmark | Common Question Stems | What Students Should Circle |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **R.2.1** Text Structure | "How does the [structure] of the passage...", "The author organizes the text by...", "What is the effect of [text feature]..." | Structure words (cause/effect, compare/contrast, chronological), feature names |
| **R.2.2** Central Idea | "What is the central idea of...", "How does [detail] support the central idea...", "Which statement best expresses..." | "central idea", "main idea", "primarily about" |
| **R.2.3** Purpose | "What is the author's purpose in...", "The author includes [element] in order to...", "How does the author's perspective influence..." | "purpose", "in order to", "perspective", "the author" |
| **R.2.4** Argument | "How does the author support the claim that...", "Which evidence best supports...", "How does the counterclaim in ¶___ affect..." | "claim", "argument", "evidence", "counterclaim", "support" |
| **R.3.4** Rhetoric | "How does the author's use of [device] appeal to...", "The rhetorical effect of ___ is...", "Which rhetorical strategy does the author employ..." | "rhetorical", "appeal", "ethos/pathos/logos", "persuade" |

## Teaching Sequence: Day 2 of Test Prep Cycle

### I Do (10-12 min at Teacher-Led Center)

**Script:**
"Yesterday you did the cold read and answered 10 MC questions. Today we're going to become EXPERTS on what those questions were actually asking. Watch what I do with Question 1."

1. **Display the question stem** (without answer choices)
2. **Apply C-U-B:** "I circle 'figurative language' and 'mood' — those are my hunt terms. I underline 'How does the author's use of' — that tells me I'm explaining the FUNCTION of a technique. I box 'lines 5-7' — that's WHERE to look."
3. **Categorize:** "This is an ANALYSIS question — it's asking HOW something works, not just WHAT it says. The benchmark is R.3.1 Figurative Language & Mood."
4. **Write hunt prediction:** "I need to find figurative language in lines 5-7 and explain what mood it creates."

**Key teaching point:** "The question stem tells you EXACTLY what to hunt for. If you skip this step, you're reading blind."

### We Do (10-12 min)

Work through 2-3 questions together:
- Students C-U-B on their own copy
- Call on students: "What did you circle? What does that tell you?"
- Class discusses: "What benchmark is this testing?"
- Students write hunt predictions, share with partner

**Common misconception to address:** Students often circle answer-choice language instead of stem language. Redirect: "We're looking at the QUESTION, not the answers yet."

### You Do with Partner (10-12 min)

Students work in pairs on remaining questions:
- C-U-B each question
- Categorize as Literal / Inference / Analysis / Evaluation
- Identify the benchmark
- Write a hunt prediction (3-5 words)

**Circulating prompts:**
- "What did you circle? Why?"
- "Is this asking what the text SAYS or what it MEANS?"
- "Which benchmark is this testing? How do you know?"
- "What's your hunt prediction?"

### You Do Independently (at Independent Center)

Students complete the Question Stem Analysis Worksheet (see template below).

## Signal Word Quick Reference (Student-Facing)

```
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| QUESTION STEM DECODER                                              |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| "According to..." / "The text states..." → LITERAL (find it)     |
| "What can be inferred..." / "suggests..." → INFERENCE (figure it) |
| "How does..." / "serves to..." / "contributes..." → ANALYSIS      |
| "Which best..." / "most effectively..." → EVALUATION (judge it)   |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
| BENCHMARK CLUES:                                                   |
| Character/setting/plot → R.1.1    Theme/message → R.1.2          |
| Narrator/perspective → R.1.3     Ambiguity/layers → R.1.4        |
| Structure/organize → R.2.1      Central idea → R.2.2             |
| Purpose/in order to → R.2.3     Claim/argument → R.2.4           |
| Figurative/mood → R.3.1         Rhetorical/appeal → R.3.4        |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

## Common Student Errors in Stem Analysis

| Error | What It Looks Like | How to Fix |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Skipping stem analysis** | Goes straight to answer choices | Enforce: "Cover the answers. Read the stem only." |
| **Circling too much** | Entire question highlighted | Redirect: "Circle ONLY the key terms that tell you WHAT to find" |
| **Confusing question type** | Calls an inference question "literal" | Practice: "Can you point to the EXACT sentence? No? Then it's inference." |
| **Wrong benchmark ID** | Labels a theme question as "literary elements" | Anchor chart: Post the benchmark-to-stem map on the wall |
| **Vague hunt prediction** | Writes "find evidence" | Model: "That's too vague. What KIND of evidence? Be specific in 3-5 words." |
| **Reading answer choices first** | Skips Phase 1 entirely | Physical barrier: fold paper to cover choices until stem analysis is done |

## ESOL Modifications

**Levels 1-2:**
- Provide bilingual signal word reference card
- Pre-teach question vocabulary: "infer," "contribute," "primarily," "establish"
- Allow students to write hunt predictions in L1
- Reduce to 5 questions for stem analysis practice
- Partner with strong English reader

**Level 3:**
- Full worksheet with sentence frames for hunt predictions:
  - "I need to find ___ in paragraph ___."
  - "This question is asking HOW ___ affects ___."
- Word bank for benchmark identification

**Levels 4-5:**
- Standard expectations
- May need vocabulary support for academic stems only ("serves to," "contributes to")
