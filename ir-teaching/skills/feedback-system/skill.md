# IR Feedback System

## Overview

**Workflow:** Practice in class (Days 1-6) → Feedback → Revise at home → Submit online (graded)

**Core Principle:** Students practice all work in class (not graded), receive formative feedback using structured forms, then revise and submit online for summative grading.

**Tool:** IR-Feedback-Form.docx (printable checklist + verbal feedback sections)

---

## Feedback Prioritization Framework

### Days 1-2: Foundation Feedback
**Focus:** Comprehension & Vocabulary

**Priority 1 (Must Address):**
- Reading comprehension errors (misunderstanding text)
- Context clue strategy use (using sentence vs. guessing)

**Priority 2 (Address if time):**
- Vocabulary accuracy (close vs. completely wrong)
- Answer justification quality (process of elimination use)

**Priority 3 (Let it go):**
- Spelling/grammar in definitions
- Handwriting/organization

**Rationale:** If students misunderstand the text or don't use context clue strategies, everything else collapses.

---

### Days 3-4: Application Feedback
**Focus:** Benchmark Organizer Skills

**Priority 1 (Must Address):**
- Benchmark-aligned thinking (doing what benchmark requires)
- Text evidence citation (paragraph numbers, relevant quotes)
- Understanding of I Do model (can they replicate the process)

**Priority 2 (Address if time):**
- Evidence quality (strong vs. weak support)
- Analysis depth (surface vs. thorough)
- Partner collaboration quality

**Priority 3 (Let it go):**
- Complete sentences (sentence starters acceptable)
- Handwriting/neatness
- All rows filled (focus on quality over quantity)

**Rationale:** Gradual release days - students must understand HOW to do the benchmark skill before doing it independently.

---

### Days 5-6: Assessment Feedback
**Focus:** Independence & Transfer

**Priority 1 (Must Address):**
- STOP protocol application (can they justify answers)
- CER/RACE structure (claim, evidence, reasoning present)
- Benchmark mastery (demonstrated skill independently)

**Priority 2 (Address if time):**
- Answer correctness (WHY wrong matters more than THAT wrong)
- Evidence quality and relevance
- Reasoning depth

**Priority 3 (Let it go):**
- Grammar/spelling (unless obscures meaning)
- Length (as long as components present)

**Rationale:** Assessing independence - can they apply the process without scaffolds?

## Feedback Form Components

**Location:** Desktop/IR-Feedback-Form.docx (print and attach to student work)

### Three Stage Checklists:

**1. Days 1-2: Comprehension & Vocabulary**
- Bellringer rating boxes (✓/○/✗ for each word)
- Comprehension question rating (✓/△/✗)
- Action step fields (which paragraphs to reread, what to look for)
- Verbal feedback notes section (5 lines for student transcription)

**2. Days 3-4: Organizer Feedback**
- Benchmark skill application checkboxes (I Do/We Do/You Do rows)
- Text evidence checklist (paragraph numbers, relevance, location)
- Analysis depth checklist (explains HOW, uses benchmark language)
- Strongest row identification + one row to improve with specific fix
- Verbal feedback notes section (5 lines)

**3. Days 5-6: Assessment Feedback**
- Score field (practice score, not recorded yet)
- STOP protocol checklist (identified Proven, eliminated S/T/O)
- CER/RACE point breakdown (__/2 claim, __/3 evidence, __/3 reasoning)
- Missed questions field + paragraphs to reread
- "Add" and "Revise" action steps
- Verbal feedback notes section (5 lines)

**4. Student Action Plan (completed before leaving Day 6)**
- 3 specific revision commitments
- Student signature + date
- Parent signature (optional)

## Implementation: When to Give Feedback

### Days 1-2: Quick Checks During Independent Rotation

**When:** During Day 2 Independent rotation

**How:**
1. Print 5-8 feedback forms
2. Circulate with clipboard during Independent
3. Spot-check bellringers (30 seconds per student - scan for ✓/○/✗)
4. Spot-check comprehension questions (1 minute per student)
5. Check boxes, write paragraph numbers for rereading
6. Hand form to student, give 15-30 seconds verbal feedback while they write

**Time:** 2-3 minutes per student = 20-30 minutes during rotation

**Example verbal feedback:**
- "Day 1 bellringer was strong - you used context clues. Day 2 word 3 needs work. Reread paragraph 5 and look for the time clue."
- "Questions 1-4 correct. Question 5 wrong - go back to paragraph 12 and look for what the character does, not says."

**Why this works:** Students get feedback on foundational skills while they still have Days 3-6 to apply corrections.

---

### Days 3-4: Organizer Checks After Gradual Release

**When:** End of Day 4 or beginning of Day 5

**How:**
1. Collect completed organizers at end of Day 4
2. Review 8-10 organizers during planning or after school
3. Attach feedback form, check boxes (2 minutes per organizer)
4. Identify strongest row and one row to improve
5. Return Day 5 with 1-minute verbal feedback per student

**Time:** 2-3 minutes checking + 1 minute verbal = 25-35 minutes for 10 students

**Example verbal feedback:**
- "I Do and We Do rows show you understand. Row 3 needs more evidence - look at Row 1 and do that same thing. Add a quote from paragraph 18."
- "Row 2 is strongest - that's proficient. Row 4, you summarized instead of analyzing. Explain HOW the evidence proves the theme, not just what happens."

**Why this works:** Students see where thinking is strong and get ONE specific correction before Days 5-6 assessment.

---

### Days 5-6: Assessment Feedback with Revision Opportunity

**When:** Day 6 or after unit ends

**How:**
1. Grade assessments with feedback form attached
2. Check boxes, assign points (3-4 minutes per assessment)
3. Write ONE specific "Add" or "Revise" action step
4. Return with 30-second verbal feedback
5. Students complete Action Plan before leaving

**Time:** 3-4 minutes per assessment = 30-40 minutes for 10 students

**Example verbal feedback:**
- "STOP analysis was strong - identified Proven correctly. CER needs more reasoning. You have claim and evidence but didn't explain HOW evidence proves claim. Look at organizer Row 2 - that reasoning goes here."
- "You missed questions 3 and 5. Both about theme development. Reread paragraphs 14 and 22 - look for how character's actions show theme changing."

**Why this works:** Students get scored feedback + actionable steps. If you allow revision, they practice using feedback to improve score.

## Student Accountability System

### Strategy 1: Feedback Form = Required Attachment

**Add to online assignment instructions:**

📌 **REQUIRED for full credit:**
1. Submit completed work (Google Doc, PDF, etc.)
2. **Upload photo/scan of feedback form** showing:
   - Teacher's checkmarks and comments
   - Your verbal feedback notes (what you wrote down)
   - Your completed Student Action Plan (3 specific actions)

**Grading penalty:**
- Missing feedback form = automatic 10% deduction
- Blank verbal feedback section = automatic 5% deduction

**Rationale:** Students must keep and refer to feedback form. Can't submit without it.

---

### Strategy 2: Student Action Plan (End of Day 6)

**Before students leave class on Day 6:**

Students complete this section on their feedback form:

```
STUDENT ACTION PLAN
Based on the feedback above, I will:

1. ___________________________________________________________
2. ___________________________________________________________
3. ___________________________________________________________
```

**Examples of good action plans:**
- Add a quote from paragraph 18 to organizer Row 3
- Revise my CER reasoning to explain HOW the evidence proves the theme
- Fix bellringer Day 2 words 3 and 5 - reread paragraphs 5 and 12

**At home:** Students use this as revision checklist, initial each when complete.

**Rationale:** Students commit to specific revisions before leaving. Accountability starts in class.

---

### Strategy 3: Self-Assessment Cover Sheet

**Students submit this with revised work:**

```
REVISION SELF-ASSESSMENT

Name: _______________ Unit: _______________ Date: ___________

I received feedback on my practice work in class. I have revised my work
based on that feedback. Here's what I changed:

1. ___________________________________________________________
   (What did you revise? Where in your work did you make this change?)

2. ___________________________________________________________

3. ___________________________________________________________

I reviewed my feedback form before submitting: □ YES

My practice score was: _____ / _____
I expect my revised score to be: _____ / _____ (based on improvements)

□ I have attached my feedback form with teacher comments
□ I completed my Student Action Plan (3 action items)
□ I made the revisions listed above
```

**Rationale:** Forces reflection on what changed. You can verify: Did they follow feedback or ignore it?

---

### Strategy 4: Feedback Response Grade Component

**Add to gradebook:** "Feedback Response" (10-15% of unit grade)

**How students earn points:**
- Completed feedback form attached to online submission: **50%**
- Student Action Plan completed with 3 specific items: **25%**
- Self-assessment cover sheet showing revisions made: **25%**

**Rationale:** Rewards USING feedback, not just receiving it. Students who ignore feedback lose easy points.

## Efficiency Tips

### Rotate Who Gets Detailed Feedback
- Don't try to feedback every student every day
- Day 1-2: Feedback 8 students on bellringers/comprehension
- Day 3-4: Feedback 10 students on organizers
- Day 5-6: Feedback 12 students on assessments
- Over 2-3 units, cycle through everyone

### Use Class Time - Don't Take Work Home
- Circulate during Independent rotation with clipboard + forms
- Give verbal feedback while students work
- No after-school grading of practice work

### Grade Only Final Online Submissions
- Practice in class = formative (feedback only, no grade)
- Online submission = summative (graded)
- One grading session per unit instead of daily

### Students Are Responsible for Feedback Form
- They lose points if they don't keep it or upload it
- Shifts burden: students must track and use feedback

## Weekly Timeline Summary

| Day | Student Action | Teacher Action | Time per Student |
|-----|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Week 1: Days 1-2** | Practice bellringers, comprehension | Spot-check 5-8 students | 2-3 min |
| **Week 1: Days 3-4** | Practice organizers | Review 8-10 organizers | 3-4 min |
| **Week 1: Days 5-6** | Practice assessment, Action Plan | Review assessments, give practice scores | 3-4 min |
| **Weekend/Week 2** | Revise at home using form | None | 0 min |
| **Week 2** | Submit online with form + self-assessment | Grade final submissions | Normal grading |

**Total feedback time per student per unit:** 8-11 minutes during class rotations

## Integration with Unit Building

When building units, include feedback system:

**In Lesson Plans:**
- Add "Feedback Timing" section for each day
- Days 1-2: "Spot-check 5-8 students during IND rotation"
- Days 3-4: "Collect organizers end of Day 4, return with feedback Day 5"
- Days 5-6: "Return assessments with feedback forms, students complete Action Plan"

**In Student Packets:**
- Add reminder at top: "This is PRACTICE work. You will receive feedback and revise before submitting online."
- Include space for feedback form attachment (staple here note)

**In Online Assignment:**
- Include feedback form upload requirement
- Include self-assessment cover sheet template
- Set "Feedback Response" category weight (10-15%)

## Quality Indicators

**Effective feedback when:**
- Students can answer: "What do I need to improve and how?"
- Revisions between practice and final submission are visible
- Students complete Action Plans with specific, actionable items
- Students keep and upload feedback forms (not losing them)
- Feedback takes 8-11 minutes per student across the week

**Ineffective feedback when:**
- Students say "I don't know what to fix"
- No difference between practice work and final submission
- Action Plans are vague ("do better", "try harder")
- Students lose feedback forms or forget to upload
- Feedback takes more than 15 minutes per student per unit
