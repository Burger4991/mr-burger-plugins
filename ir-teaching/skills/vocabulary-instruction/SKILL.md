# Vocabulary Instruction Skill

Academic vocabulary selection and instruction strategies for 10th grade Intensive Reading, aligned with Florida BEST ELA.10.V.1 (vocabulary acquisition and use).

## Tier System for Vocabulary Selection

### Tier 1: Basic/Conversational Words
- Common everyday words
- Usually learned through oral language
- **Do NOT teach explicitly** in 10th grade IR
- Examples: happy, big, run, house

### Tier 2: Academic/High-Utility Words
- **PRIORITY for Intensive Reading**
- Appear across content areas
- Essential for academic success
- Worth teaching explicitly
- Students unlikely to learn independently

**Examples:**
- analyze, contribute, demonstrate, establish
- contrast, infer, interpret, determine
- significant, relevant, coherent, convey

**How to Identify Tier 2:**
- Appears in multiple texts/contexts
- Important for comprehension
- Can be explained using known words
- Will be useful in future reading

### Tier 3: Domain-Specific/Technical Words
- Specific to a topic or field
- Teach in context when needed
- Less transferable
- Examples: photosynthesis, metaphor, algorithm

**When to Teach Tier 3 in IR:**
- Literary terms (symbolism, foreshadowing, irony)
- Only when essential for text comprehension
- Only when benchmark requires it (e.g., ELA.10.R.2.1 - figurative language)

## Vocabulary Selection Criteria

### For Each Unit, Select 5-7 Words That:

✅ **Are Tier 2** (high-utility academic words)
✅ **Appear in the unit text** (teach in context)
✅ **Support benchmark understanding** (e.g., "theme" words for theme unit)
✅ **Are grade-appropriate** (not too easy, not impossibly hard)
✅ **Have instructional potential** (interesting, useful, teachable)

### RED FLAGS - Don't Select Words That:
❌ Students definitely already know
❌ Only appear once and aren't useful elsewhere
❌ Are proper nouns or names
❌ Are too simple for 10th grade
❌ Can't be explained without extremely technical knowledge

## Vocabulary Instruction Framework

### Day 1: Introduction (Teacher-Led, 10 minutes)

**1. Present Word in Context**
- Show sentence from text
- Read aloud with expression
- Students identify word from context clues

**2. Student-Friendly Definition**
- Explain in simple language
- Connect to students' experiences
- NOT just dictionary definition

**Example:**
- Word: **persevere**
- Context: "Despite failing three times, she persevered until she succeeded."
- Student-Friendly: "To persevere means to keep trying even when something is really hard or you want to give up."

**3. Visual/Gesture**
- Show image or act it out
- Creates memory hook
- Especially important for ESOL

**4. Examples & Non-Examples**
- Give 2-3 examples
- Give 1-2 non-examples
- Students identify which is which

**Example:**
- "Studying for a test even though you're tired—is that persevering?" (YES)
- "Quitting basketball because practice is hard—is that persevering?" (NO)

### Day 2: Deep Processing (Independent Work, 15 minutes)

**Vocabulary Practice Activities:**

**Frayer Model:**
```
┌─────────────────┬─────────────────┐
│   Definition    │    Examples     │
│  (in own words) │                 │
├─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
│  Non-Examples   │    Synonyms     │
│                 │   or Related    │
└─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
         Word: ____________
```

**Semantic Mapping:**
- Word in center circle
- Related words, synonyms, examples branch out
- Visual connections

**Vocabulary Journals:**
- Word + part of speech
- Student-friendly definition
- Sentence from text (with paragraph #)
- Original sentence
- Drawing/visual

**Word Sorts:**
- Give 10-15 words
- Students categorize by meaning, part of speech, or connotation
- Discuss reasoning

### Days 3-4: Application

**In Organizers:**
- Sentence frames using vocabulary
- "The author uses [vocabulary word] to show..."

**In Discussion:**
- Require use of 2-3 vocabulary words
- "Academic language challenge"

**Writing Integration:**
- Use vocabulary in RACE responses
- Check off which words were used

### Days 5-6: Assessment

**Matching:**
```
1. persevere        A. to keep trying despite difficulty
2. consequence      B. result of an action
3. abandon          C. to give up on something
```

**Context Application:**
Choose the sentence that uses the word correctly:
```
○ A. I will persevere my homework tonight. (incorrect usage)
○ B. She persevered through the difficult math problem. (correct)
○ C. The persevere was very tall. (nonsense)
○ D. He asked me to persevere the book. (incorrect meaning)
```

**Original Sentence:**
Write a sentence using the word correctly:
```
persevere: ________________________________________
         (must show understanding of meaning)
```

## Teaching Strategies by Word Type

### Abstract Words (theme, perspective, impact)

**Strategy:**
- Use concrete examples from students' lives first
- Build to text examples
- Graphic organizers
- Compare/contrast with related words

**Example for "perspective":**
1. "Think of a time you and a friend disagreed about a movie. You had different _____ (perspectives)"
2. "In the text, how do the two characters have different perspectives on the problem?"

### Process/Action Words (analyze, infer, determine)

**Strategy:**
- Model the process
- Think-aloud
- Students practice with scaffolds
- Use consistently across units

**Example for "analyze":**
1. "Watch me analyze this quote: First I look for key words, then I think about what they mean, then I connect to the theme..."
2. "Now you analyze this next quote with your partner."

### Emotionally-Loaded Words (persevere, abandon, triumph)

**Strategy:**
- Personal connections
- Role-play or scenarios
- Identify in texts (characters who persevere)
- Emotional scale (which is stronger: persevere vs. try?)

## ESOL Vocabulary Strategies

### For Beginning ELLs:
- Cognates (Spanish: perseverar = persevere)
- Visuals for every word
- Translation if needed (strategic, not constant)
- Word walls with images
- Gestures/TPR (Total Physical Response)

### For Intermediate ELLs:
- Sentence frames using new words
- Word families (persevere, perseverance, persevering)
- Multiple exposures (7-12 times to acquire)
- Peer practice with sentence stems

### For Advanced ELLs:
- Nuance discussions (persevere vs. persist)
- Register (formal vs. informal usage)
- Idiomatic expressions
- Independent word learning strategies

## Vocabulary Instructional Routines

### "Word of the Day" (3 minutes):
- Quick review of one vocabulary word
- Students use in a sentence
- Teacher provides context
- Builds in spaced repetition

### "Academic Language Challenge":
- Students earn points for using vocabulary in discussion
- Keep tally on board
- Celebrate academic language use

### "Vocabulary Police":
- Student job: listen for classmates using vocabulary words
- Ring bell or give signal
- Class acknowledges ("Nice use of 'analyze', Marcus!")

### Word Consciousness:
- Notice words in other classes, media, life
- Share sightings
- Builds awareness and ownership

## Vocabulary Resources to Create

### Word Bank Handouts:
- Organized by benchmark or unit
- Includes definition, example sentence, visual
- Student reference during independent work

### Personal Vocabulary Journals:
- Student-created
- Pages for each word
- Definition, sentence, drawing, connection
- Review for assessments

### Vocabulary Anchor Charts:
- Posted in classroom
- Word + visual + example
- Build across units
- Students refer to during work

## Assessment of Vocabulary

### Formative:
- Daily use in discussion (observation)
- Check vocabulary journals
- Quick matching quizzes
- Sentence correction tasks

### Summative (in unit assessments):
- Multiple choice context questions
- Matching definitions
- Original sentence writing
- RACE responses that demonstrate word understanding

### Rubric for Original Sentences:

**2 Points:**
- Uses word correctly
- Shows clear understanding of meaning
- Complete, coherent sentence

**1 Point:**
- Attempts to use word
- Partially correct meaning
- Sentence issues (fragment, unclear)

**0 Points:**
- Incorrect usage
- Misunderstands meaning
- No attempt

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ Teaching too many words (7+ overwhelms)
❌ Dictionary definitions only (not student-friendly)
❌ One-time introduction (need 7-12 exposures)
❌ No visual support (especially critical for ESOL)
❌ Teaching out of context (always use text examples)
❌ Testing on Friday, never using again (need spiraling)
❌ Assuming students "should know" words (don't assume)

## Integration with Benchmark Instruction

### For Theme Units:
- Vocabulary: theme, convey, demonstrate, illustrate, reveal
- Focus on "theme language"

### For Central Idea Units:
- Vocabulary: central, support, develop, establish, emphasize
- Focus on "main idea language"

### For Text Structure Units:
- Vocabulary: sequence, compare, contrast, cause, effect
- Focus on "organization language"

### For Figurative Language Units:
- Vocabulary: literal, figurative, symbolize, metaphor, imply
- Focus on "literary language"

## Quick Reference: 5-Step Vocabulary Lesson

1. **Context** - Show word in text sentence
2. **Definition** - Student-friendly explanation
3. **Visual** - Image, gesture, or example
4. **Practice** - Students use it (speaking/writing)
5. **Review** - Spiral back multiple times
