# Collaborative Discussion Protocols

Active learning strategies requiring genuine participation in discussions.

## Protocol Selection

| Protocol | Time | Best For | Day |
|----------|:----:|----------|:---:|
| Think-Pair-Share | 5-10 min | Quick discussions, activating knowledge | Any |
| Socratic Seminar | 20-30 min | Deep text analysis, critical thinking | Days 2, 4, 6 |

---

## THINK-PAIR-SHARE

**Time:** 5-10 minutes | **Grouping:** Partners

### Structure

**THINK (2 min) - Silent Individual Work**
- Pose complex question (on board)
- Timer visible: 2 minutes
- Students write 3-4 sentences minimum
- Must include text evidence

**Question Examples:**
- Theme: "What theme is revealed in this scene? Evidence?"
- Purpose: "What is the author's main purpose? Find evidence."
- Rhetoric: "How does the author use rhetoric to persuade?"
- Argument: "Do you agree with the author's argument? Why?"

**PAIR (3-5 min) - Partner Discussion**
- Partner A speaks first (1-2 min)
- Partner B listens (no interrupting)
- Switch roles
- Discuss together (1 min): "What did we agree/disagree on?"

**Sentence Frames:**
- "I agree with you because..."
- "I disagree because..."
- "My evidence is... What's yours?"

**SHARE (2-3 min) - Whole Class**
- Call on 3-5 pairs randomly
- "What did you and your partner conclude?"
- Chart responses
- Push: "Why?" "Evidence?"

### Modifications

**Struggling Students:** Sentence frames, word bank, pair with stronger partner
**Advanced:** DOK 3-4 questions, multiple evidence, cross-text connections
**ELL:** Preview question day before, allow native language first, extended think time

## SOCRATIC SEMINAR

**Time:** 20-30 minutes | **Grouping:** Inner circle (8-10) + Outer circle (observers)

### Setup
- Two circles: Inner (discussants) + Outer (observers/note-takers)
- Teacher: Facilitator (not lecturer)

### Phase 1: Opening Question (5 min)
- Pose open-ended question
- Examples: "Is Obama's rhetoric effective?" "Does the author's purpose change?"
- Step back, let students lead

**Rules:**
- Cite text for every claim
- Respond to previous speaker before adding new idea
- Respectful disagreement encouraged
- No hand-raising (natural flow)

### Phase 2: Student-Led Discussion (15-20 min)

**Inner Circle Roles:**
1. **Discussion Leader** - Keeps conversation moving, calls on quiet students
2. **Text Tracker** - Writes evidence on board, tracks ¶ numbers
3. **Devil's Advocate** - Challenges ideas respectfully
4. **Connector** - Links ideas between students
5. **Participants** - Share ideas, cite evidence

**Outer Circle Tasks:**
- Track discussion on graphic organizer
- Count speaking turns
- Note strongest evidence
- Identify unanswered questions

### Phase 3: Debrief (5 min)
- Outer circle shares observations
- Inner circle reflects: "What went well? What was challenging?"
- Optional: Switch circles halfway

### Scaffolds

**Talking Chips:** Each student gets 2 chips, must use both (forces equal participation)

**Sentence Frames:**
- "I agree with [name] because..."
- "The text says [quote], which shows..."
- "I want to build on what [name] said..."

**Pre-Discussion Prep:** Give questions in advance, students write responses first

### Materials
- Desks in circles
- Discussion questions (3-5 open-ended)
- Observation sheets for outer circle
- Sentence frames poster
- Talking chips (optional)
- Text access during discussion
