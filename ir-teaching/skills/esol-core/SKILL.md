# ESOL Strategies - Complete Guide

## Daily Minimums (REQUIRED)

Every day must include:
- **2+ ESOL strategies** (rotate different strategies, don't repeat same ones daily)
- **1+ visual organizer** (graphic organizers, charts, diagrams)
- **1+ modification** (for assessments and complex tasks)

## Student Population

**10th Grade Intensive Reading typically includes:**
- Mixed-ability groups (High/Medium/Low)
- ESE students distributed across groups by ability level
- ESOL students (varying proficiency: Level 1-5)
- NOT tracked by label, tracked by skill need

**Differentiation Method:**
- Differentiate PROCESS, not content (UDL principles)
- MAINTAIN RIGOR - increase support, don't reduce difficulty
- Gradually release scaffolds by Day 5-6 per benchmark descriptors

## Top 8 Core Strategies (Rotate These)

### 1. Partner Reading
**When to use:** Days 1-2 (first read), Tier 3 students throughout

**How:**
- Pair students strategically (stronger reader + developing reader)
- Alternate paragraphs or pages
- Pause for margin questions together
- Discuss comprehension checks

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Partner Read with mixed-ability pairs"

### 2. Sentence Frames
**When to use:** All writing tasks, organizers, discussions

**How:**
- Provide sentence starters for responses
- Examples:
  - "The theme is __________ because __________."
  - "In paragraph ___, the author shows __________ by __________."
  - "The evidence supports this claim because __________."

**Progression:**
- Days 1-2: Full sentence frames (fill in the blank)
- Days 3-4: Sentence starters only (students complete thought)
- Days 5-6: Remove frames (independent writing)

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Sentence frames for CER responses"

### 3. Choral Reading
**When to use:** Vocabulary introduction, complex text passages, poetry

**How:**
- Teacher reads first (model fluency and pronunciation)
- Class reads aloud together
- Builds confidence for struggling readers
- Reinforces pronunciation of academic vocabulary

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Choral reading of vocabulary sentences"

### 4. Word Walls / Vocabulary Lists
**When to use:** Daily, building throughout unit

**How:**
- Post Tier 2 academic vocabulary on wall or anchor chart
- Include: word, student-friendly definition, visual, example sentence
- Reference during instruction: "Remember our word 'persevere' from the wall?"
- Students use for writing tasks

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Reference word wall during writing"

### 5. Visuals & Graphics
**When to use:** Concept introduction, vocabulary, text structure

**How:**
- Show images related to theme/topic
- Use diagrams for text structure (plot diagram, cause/effect chart)
- Include visuals on slides and in packets
- Gestures and TPR (Total Physical Response) for vocabulary

**Examples:**
- Theme = show image representing the theme concept
- Setting = show picture of time period/location
- Conflict = use diagram showing internal vs. external

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Visuals for theme concepts (freedom, oppression)"

### 6. Think-Write-Pair-Share (TWPS)
**When to use:** Checking comprehension, discussion prep, analysis tasks

**How:**
- **Think:** Students reflect individually (1 min)
- **Write:** Students write response (2-3 min)
- **Pair:** Students share with partner (2 min)
- **Share:** Volunteers share with class (2-3 min)

**Why it works for ESOL:**
- Writing time allows processing in both languages
- Partner practice before public speaking
- Rehearses academic language

**Label in lesson plan:** "Engagement: TWPS for theme identification (ESOL support: writing time)"

### 7. Simplified/Chunked Directions
**When to use:** All student-facing tasks

**How:**
- Break multi-step directions into numbered steps
- Use one verb per step
- Bold key action words (read, circle, underline, write)
- Show example of completed task

**Example:**
```
Instead of: "Read the passage and answer the questions using text evidence."

Use:
1. **Read** the passage on pages 2-3
2. **Underline** key details as you read
3. **Answer** questions 1-5
4. **Cite** paragraph numbers for each answer
```

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Numbered, chunked directions"

### 8. Translation & Cognates
**When to use:** Vocabulary instruction, complex concepts

**How:**
- Identify cognates (words similar in English and Spanish/other languages)
  - Example: "analyze" (English) = "analizar" (Spanish)
  - Example: "theme" = "tema"
- Allow students to use translation apps for key vocabulary (not full text)
- Teach false cognates (words that look similar but mean different things)

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Identify cognates for academic vocabulary"

## Extended Strategy Bank (Additional Techniques)

### 9. Margin Annotations Guide
**When to use:** Days 1-2 during Independent
**How:**
- Pre-mark margins with symbols:
  - Star = Important idea
  - ? = Confusing part
  - Lightbulb = Connection to theme
- ESOL students know exactly what to mark
- Reduces cognitive load

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Pre-marked annotation symbols for first read"

### 10. Sentence Unpacking
**When to use:** Days 3-4 (for complex sentences)
**How:**
- Take complex sentence from text
- Break into chunks
- Color-code:
  - **WHO** (subject) = blue
  - **DID WHAT** (action) = green
  - **WHEN/WHERE/WHY** = yellow

**Example:**
"Despite countless failures, she refused to abandon her dream."
- **WHO**: she
- **DID WHAT**: refused to abandon her dream
- **WHEN/WHY**: Despite countless failures

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Sentence unpacking for complex syntax"

### 11. Collaborative Annotation
**When to use:** Days 3-4 (text analysis)
**How:**
- Small groups share one text
- Each student assigned a color
- Annotate for different purposes:
  - Student 1: Theme evidence (blue)
  - Student 2: Character actions (green)
  - Student 3: Important quotes (yellow)
- Discuss findings together

**Label in lesson plan:** "ESOL: Collaborative annotation with color-coded roles"

## Modifications for Assessments

### Reading Modifications
- **Read-aloud option:** Teacher or peer reads questions aloud
- **Extended time:** +50% time for completion
- **Simplified language:** Reword complex questions (maintain rigor)
- **Chunking:** Break assessment into smaller sections

### Writing Modifications
- **Sentence frames:** Provide for constructed responses (see Strategy #2)
- **Word banks:** Provide key vocabulary for writing tasks
- **Graphic organizers:** Pre-structured templates for responses
- **Reduced length:** Shorter paragraph (3-5 sentences vs. 7-10)

### Assessment Modifications
- **Reduced items:** Fewer questions, same rigor (10 questions → 7 questions)
- **Oral administration:** Student responds verbally, teacher scribes
- **Reference materials allowed:** Can use word wall, notes, organizer
- **Testing in small group:** Separate setting with teacher support

## Engagement Strategies (Support ESOL Learners)

### Cold-Call with Support
- **Pre-cue:** "Marcus, I'm going to call on you next about the theme"
- Gives ESOL students processing time
- Reduces anxiety of surprise call

### Academic Discussion Stems
Provide sentence frames for discussions:
- "I agree with _______ because _______."
- "I respectfully disagree because _______."
- "Building on what _______ said, _______."
- "My evidence is different. I found _______."

### Turn-and-Talk
- Quick 30-second partner check-ins
- Lower stakes than whole-class share
- Practice academic language

## Grouping Strategies

### Ability-Based Groups (for rotations)
- **High:** Can work more independently, extend with deeper questions
- **Medium:** Benefit from some scaffolds and periodic check-ins
- **Low:** Need maximum support, frequent CFUs, more teacher proximity

**CRITICAL:** Don't group by label (ESE/ESOL)
- Group by actual skill need demonstrated through data
- ESOL Level 4-5 might be in high group
- Native English speaker might be in low group due to reading level

### Strategic Pairing
- **For reading:** Pair high with medium, medium with low
- **For discussion:** Pair students with different strengths (strong reader + strong speaker)
- **For writing:** Vary partnerships throughout unit (not always same pairs)

## Scaffolding Progression Across 6 Days

### Days 1-2: Maximum Support
- Full sentence frames
- Word banks provided
- Partner work encouraged
- Frequent check-ins
- Visuals for all new concepts

### Days 3-4: Partial Support
- Sentence starters (not full frames)
- Partial word banks
- Mix of partner and independent work
- Periodic check-ins
- Reference to previous visuals

### Days 5-6: Minimal Support (Assessment Conditions)
- No sentence frames (remove scaffolds)
- No word banks (students recall independently)
- Independent work expected
- Intervention available if student requests help
- Modifications still in place for IEP/ELL students

## Labeling in Lesson Plans

Always label ESOL strategies explicitly:

**Good examples:**
- "ESOL Strategy 1: Partner reading with mixed-ability pairs (Tier 3 students)"
- "ESOL Strategy 2: Sentence frames for theme statements"
- "Visual Support: Image of Victorian era for setting context (ESOL)"

**Poor examples:**
- "Students work in pairs" ← Not labeled as ESOL strategy
- "Provide sentence starters" ← Not specific enough
- "Differentiation for ELLs" ← Too vague

**Use this for quick planning:**

| 3-4 | Discussion | TWPS + Academic Discussion Stems |
